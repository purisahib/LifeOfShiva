### Chapter 40: Journey to Kailasa and the Vision of Siva
*(Rudra-samhita, Sati-khanda — fetched from wisdomlib)*

**Context:** After Virabhadra destroyed Daksha's yajna, the defeated gods seek Shiva at Kailash.

**Description of Kailash (verses 23-37):**
- Multiple peaks adorned with precious gems
- Diverse celestial flora: trees and creepers
- Wildlife "free from cruelty"
- Sacred rivers: the Ganga, Nanda, and Alakananda
- The divine Saugandhika park (fragrant garden)
- Alaka — the city of Kubera
- A sacred fig-tree (Vata) spanning "a hundred Yojanas" where Siva practices yoga

**Siva's appearance (verses 37-42):**
- Seated beneath the Vata tree
- Attended by Brahma's sons, great Siddhas, Kubera and his attendants
- Ashes (Bhasma) smeared across his body
- Rudraksa (Rudraksha) garland suspended from his wrist
- Left leg positioned over right thigh
- Displaying the **Tarka-mudra** gesture (indicating philosophical discourse/teaching)

**The Meeting (verses 43-46):**
- Visnu and the deities perform obeisance (prostration) to Siva
- Siva stands and reciprocates with equal respect
- Gods then formally address Siva

### Chapter 41: Devas Eulogise Siva
*(Rudra-samhita, Sati-khanda — fetched from wisdomlib)*

**Verses 1-3:** The devas acknowledge Siva as supreme Brahman. They express puzzlement at his use of divine illusion (maya) while recognizing him as beyond both Prakrti (material nature) and Purusa (consciousness).

**Verses 4-6:** The devas recognize Siva's three cosmic roles: creator, sustainer, and destroyer. They acknowledge he established sacrificial traditions through Daksa.

**Verses 9-12:** Praise of Siva as supreme creator and sustainer.

**Verses 14-41 — Enumeration of Siva's Forms:**
- Omkara (Om) and Vasatkara sacred utterances
- His representation as the five elements (earth, water, fire, wind, ether)
- His various epithets:
  - **Rudra**
  - **Bhairava**
  - **Mrtyunjaya** (conqueror of death)
  - **Astamurtti** (eight-form manifestation)
- The legendary act of swallowing the **Kalakata poison** during the churning of the cosmic ocean

**Verses 45-52 — The Devas' Petition:**
The devas formally request Siva to:
- Restore the incomplete sacrifice of Daksa
- Heal the injuries inflicted on the gods during the conflict
- Grant the gods proper shares in future rituals
- Visnu and Brahma seek forgiveness through prostration
