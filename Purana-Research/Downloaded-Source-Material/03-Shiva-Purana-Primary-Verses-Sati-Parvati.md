## Chapter 14 — The Birth of Satī and Her Childish Sports
**URL:** https://www.wisdomlib.org/hinduism/book/shiva-purana-english/d/doc226034.html

**Verse 1:** "In the mean time, O celestial sage I, the grandfather of the worlds, came there, on hearing the incident."

**Verse 2:** "I consoled Dakṣa as before. Clever that I was I made him friendly with you."

**Verse 3:** "O best of sages, I consoled you—my own son, beloved of the Devas, and taking you with love effected conciliation."

**Verse 4:** "Then Dakṣa, consoled by me, begot of his wife sixty comely daughters."

**Verse 5:** "Without any lassitude he performed their marriages with Dharma and others."

**Verse 6:** "Dakṣa gave ten of his daughters duly in marriage to Dharma, thirteen to Kaśyapa the sage, and twenty seven to the moon."

**Verses 7-8:** "He gave two daughters each to Bhṛgu, Aṅgiras and Kṛśāśva. The other daughters were given to Tārkṣya. The sons and grandsons and descendants of these filled the three worlds."

**Verse 9:** "Some say that Śivā was the eldest of his daughters, some say that she was the middle and some say that she was the youngest. All the three opinions are correct in different Kalpas."

**Verse 10:** "After the birth of his daughter, Dakṣa Prajāpati and his wife meditated on the mother of the universe with pleasure."

**Verse 11:** "Then he lovingly eulogised her with words choked at the throat and repeatedly paid respects to her humbly joining the palms in reverence."

**Verse 12:** "The Goddess, who was highly delighted, thought within himself. 'I shall incarnate in Vīriṇī in order to keep my word'."

**Verse 13:** "O excellent sage, then the mother of the universe spoke to Dakṣa in mind. Then Dakṣa shone forth splendidly."

**Verse 14:** "In an auspicious hour he deposited his semen in his wife. Thus full of compassion, Śivā began to reside in the womb of Dakṣa's consort."

**Verse 15:** "All the characteristic signs of pregnancy appeared in her."

**Verse 16:** "Dear one, thanks to the power of the presence of Śivā, Vīriṇī had an auspicious appearance and shone all the more with mental pleasure."

**Verse 17:** "As befitting the loftiness of his mind, customs and manners current in his family and the injunctions of the Vedas, Dakṣa performed the rites of Puṃsavana etc. out of affection."

**Verse 18:** "Great festivities accompanied those rites. Dakṣa presented liberal sums of money to the brahmins."

**Verse 19:** "On coming to know that the Goddess had come to the womb of Vīriṇī, Viṣṇu and other Devas became very gay."

**Verse 20:** "They all approached her and paid frequent respects to the benefactress of the worlds, eulogising the mother of the universe."

**Verse 21:** "Delighted in their hearts they praised Dakṣa and Vīriṇī in various ways and went to their respective abodes."

**Verses 22-23:** "O Nārada, nine months had passed with due observance of worldly conventions. In the tenth month, in an auspicious happy hour, when the moon, the stars and the planets were favourably disposed, Śivā suddenly appeared, O sage, in front of her mother."

**Verse 24:** "As soon as she was born, Dakṣa was highly pleased. On seeing her extremely brilliant he was convinced that she was the Goddess Śivā herself."

**Verse 25:** "O excellent sage, when she was born there was a gentle shower from the clouds accompanied by that of flowers. The quarters became tranquil immediately."

**Verse 26:** "The devas gathered in the sky and played on musical instruments; sacrificial fires kindled calmly; everything indicated auspiciousness."

**Verse 27:** "On seeing the mother of the universe born of Vīriṇī, Dakṣa joined his palms in reverence, paid respects to her and eulogised her."

**Verse 28:** "O Goddess, the eternal mother of the universe, obeisance to Thee. O great Goddess, the Truthful and truth-featured, be pleased."

**Verse 29:** "I bow to Thee, the bestower of benefits, Thee who art auspicious, calm, great illusion, mystic slumber and identical with the universe."

**Verse 30:** "I bow to Thee, the great mother of the universe, the great Goddess, by whom formerly Brahmā had been directed to create the worlds which he carried out."

**Verse 31:** "I bow to Thee, great support of the universe, the great Goddess, by whom formerly Viṣṇu had been directed to sustain the universe which he has been doing always."

**Verse 32:** "I bow to Thee the great mother of the universe, the great Goddess, by whom formerly Rudra had been directed to annihilate the universe which he has been doing always."

**Verse 33:** "I bow to Thee, Śivā, of Rājasika Sāttvika, and Tāmasika forms, performing every thing always, and the Goddess mother of the three deities."

**Verse 34:** "O Goddess, enjoyment of worldly pleasures and salvation are always within the reach of that person who meditates on thee in the form of Vidyā and Avidyā, every day."

**Verse 35:** "O Goddess, he who directly perceives Thee, the sanctifying deity, will certainly attain salvation with the discrimination of Vidyā and Avidyā."

**Verse 36:** "O mother of the universe, those who eulogise Thee with the names of Bhavānī, Ambikā, Jaganmāyā and Durgā will have everything."

**Verse 37:** "Śivā, the mother of the universe, eulogised by Dakṣa the intelligent, said to Dakṣa without making it heard by her mother."

**Verse 38:** "Śivā, the great Goddess and the source of future creation, made everyone deluded and said in such a manner that Dakṣa heard the truth and not anyone else."

**Verse 39:** "O Prajāpati, I had been propitiated formerly for becoming your daughter. Your wish has been fulfilled now. You can carry on your activities of penance."

**Verse 40:** "Having spoken thus to Dakṣa, the Goddess assumed infancy through her illusory power and began to cry near her mother."

**Verse 41:** "On heaving the cry, the woman spoke in agitation. The servant-maids too became pleasantly agitated."

**Verse 42:** "On seeing the comely form of Asiknī's daughter, the women rejoiced. The citizens raised shouts of Victory then."

**Verse 43:** "There were great festivities with songs and musical instruments. On seeing the unearthly face of their daughter the pleasure of Dakṣa and Asiknī knew no bounds."

**Verse 44:** "Dakṣa duly performed all the conventional ceremonies and the rites of the Vedas. He gave various gifts to the brahmins and money to others."

**Verse 45:** "Songs and dances were performed everywhere in a befitting manner. Musical instruments played auspicious songs repeatedly."

**Verse 46:** "Hari and other Gods came with their attendants, along with the sages and joined the festivities."

**Verse 47:** "On seeing the daughter of Dakṣa, the Goddess mother of the universe, they bowed and eulogised her with auspicious hymns."

**Verse 48:** "In their great delight they shouted cries of victory. They praised Dakṣa and Vīriṇī in particular."

**Verse 49:** "Then at their bidding, the delighted Dakṣa named her Umā since she inherited good qualities and was greatly admired."

**Verse 50:** "Her other names in the world were assigned afterwards. They are auspicious and quell miseries in particular."

**Verse 51:** "With palms joined in reverence Dakṣa bowed to Hari, me, devas and sages. He eulogised and worshipped all."

**Verse 52:** "Then Viṣṇu and others praised Dakṣa and in joyous mood returned to their respective abodes remembering Śiva accompanied by Śivā."

**Verse 53:** "Consecrating the daughter in a befitting manner, the mother fed her with fresh milk in the usual manner of feeding infants."

**Verse 54:** "Duly nurtured by Vīriṇī and the noble-souled Dakṣa, she flourished every day like the digit of the moon in the bright half of a month."

**Verse 55:** "O excellent brahmin, even in her infancy, the good qualities entered her like all the beautiful digits entering the moon."

**Verse 56:** "While she was engaged in various sports in the midst of her girl friends, she used to draw pictures of Śiva everyday."

**Verse 57:** "Whenever she sang sweet songs as is usual in childhood, she remembered Sthāṇu, Rudra, the suppressor of Kāma."

**Verse 58:** "The Couple (Dakṣa and his wife) found her unrivalled mercy increase, even as she had been a great devotee in childhood itself."

**Verse 59:** "Endowed with qualities of childhood and making her own residence flourish she delighted her parents for ever."

## Chapter 18 — Marriage of Śiva and Satī
**URL:** https://www.wisdomlib.org/hinduism/book/shiva-purana-english/d/doc226038.html

**Nārada said:**

**Verse 1:** "When you approached Śiva, what was it that transpired? What were the events? What did Śiva Himself do?"

**Brahmā said:**

**Verse 2:** "I approached lord Śiva who was staying in the Himālayan mountains in order to bring Him (to the house of Dakṣa). I was in a joyous mood."

**Verse 3:** "On seeing me, the creator of the world, approaching, the bull-emblemed Śiva had doubts about the acquisition of Satī."

**Verse 4:** "Due to His real affection or as a part of His divine sports in conformity with the conventions of the world or due to the devotion of Satī, Śiva immediately spoke to me like an ordinary man."

**Śiva said:**

**Verse 5:** "O eldest of devas, what did your son (Dakṣa) do in the matter of Satī. Tell me lest my heart should be severed by the cupid."

**Verse 6:** "This anxiety of separation, O eldest of devas, running between Satī and me attacks only me, leaving the other, the woman, who very well sustains her life."

**Verse 7:** "O Brahmā, respect the name Satī. Let me do what shall be done. She is not different from me. She has to be attained by me. O Brahmā, act accordingly."

**Brahmā said:**

**Verse 8:** "O sage Nārada, on hearing the words of Śiva best speaking of His strict adherence to the conventions of the world I told Śiva, consoling Him."

**Verse 9:** "O bull-emblemed God, hear what my son told me regarding Satī. Rest assured that what you wanted to achieve has been achieved."

**Verse 10:** "Dakṣa has said 'My daughter shall go to Him. She has been intended for Him. This has been my desire.' Now that you also say, it is all the more necessary that it shall be carried out."

**Verse 11:** "For this purpose Śiva had been propitiated by my daughter. Now He too seeks her. Hence She has to be offered to Him by me."

**Verse 12:** "Let Him come to me in an auspicious conjunction of stars. Then, O Brahmā, I shall offer my daughter to Him in the form of Alms."

**Verse 13:** "O bull-emblemed God, Dakṣa has told me so. Go to his house in an auspicious hour and bring her here."

**Verse 14:** "O sage, on hearing these words of mine, Rudra, who is favourably disposed to His devotees, spoke with a smile, strictly adhering to the conventions of the world."

**Śiva said:**

**Verse 15:** "I shall go to his house accompanied by you and Nārada. Hence, O creator of the universe, you remember Nārada."

**Verse 16:** "Remember your mental as well as physical sons—Marīci and others. O Brahmā, with all my attendants and with them I shall go to Dakṣa's house."

**Brahmā said:**

**Verse 17:** "Thus commanded by Śiva following the conventions of the world, I remembered you, Nārada and the other sons—Marīci etc."

**Verse 18:** "Immediately after I remembered them, all of my mental sons and you arrived in a happy mood."

**Verse 19:** "Remembered by Śiva, Viṣṇu, the foremost of Śiva's devotees, came there along with the Goddess Lakṣmī seated on Garuḍa and accompanied by his army."

**Verse 20:** "In the bright half of the month of Caitra (March-April) on the thirteenth day when the star was Uttarā Phalguni on a Sunday, lord Śiva started."

**Verse 21:** "Going ahead, with all the devas, led by Brahmā and Viṣṇu and accompanied by the sages, Śiva shone brilliantly."

**Verse 22:** "Great festivities were arranged by Devas and the attendants of Śiva who were in the happiest mood, on their way."

**Verse 23:** "The hides of elephant and tiger, the serpents, the crescent moon and the matted hair, all became fitting ornaments and embellishments at Śiva's will."

**Verse 24:** "Then in a trice, Śiva reached Dakṣa's abode seated on his speedy bull and along with Viṣṇu and others."

**Verse 25:** "With great humility and boundless joy, Dakṣa along with his people welcomed Him."

**Verse 26:** "The Devas and their attendants were honoured by Dakṣa. The sages were seated in their due order."

**Verse 27:** "Then Dakṣa took Śiva within the house along with the devas and the sages."

**Verse 28:** "The delighted Dakṣa worshipped lord Śiva, after offering him an excellent seat."

**Verse 29:** "He worshipped Viṣṇu, me, the brahmins, devas and the Gaṇas of Śiva, with great devotion and in a fitting manner."

**Verse 30:** "After performing the suitable worship, Dakṣa in the presence of respectable sages announced the marriage agreement."

**Verse 31:** "Then Dakṣa, my son, knelt before me, his father, with pleasure and said—'O lord, the marriage rites shall be performed by you.'"

**Verse 32:** "Saying 'Amen' I got up with a delightful heart and performed the preliminary rites."

**Verse 33:** "Then in an auspicious conjunction of stars with the planets in a propitious position, Dakṣa joyfully gave his daughter Satī to Siva."

**Verse 34:** "As a part of the rites of marriage the delighted Śiva grasped the hand of Satī of comely appearance."

**Verse 35:** "We all, Viṣṇu, I, you and other sages, bowed to Śiva and delighted Him with laudatory hymns."

**Verse 36:** "There were great festivities with songs and dances. The sages and the devas were in a gay mood."

**Verse 37:** "After offering his daughter, Dakṣa, my son, was extremely satisfied, Satī and Śiva were in happy mood. Everything concluded auspiciously."

## Chapter 27 — The Inauguration of Dakṣa's Sacrifice
**URL:** https://www.wisdomlib.org/hinduism/book/shiva-purana-english/d/doc226047.html

**Brahmā said:**

**Verses 1-2:** "Once a great sacrifice was started by Dakṣa, O sage. To partake in that sacrifice, the celestial and terrestrial sages and devas were invited by Śiva and they reached the place being deluded by Śiva's Māyā."

**Verses 3-5:** The passage lists numerous sages including "Agastya, Kaśyapa, Atri, Vāmadeva, Bhṛgu, Dadhīci, the revered Vyāsa, Bharadvāja, Gautama" and many others who arrived at Dakṣa's sacrifice.

**Verse 6:** "All the devas, the guardians of the quarters of rising fortune, the subordinate devas with their offers of help and service attended the sacrifice."

**Verse 7:** "From Satyaloka, I, the creator of the universe, was duly lauded and taken there along with my sons, followers and the embodied forms of the Vedas etc."

**Verse 8:** "Viṣṇu was duly requested, respected and brought to the place of sacrifice from Vaikuṇṭha along with his aide-de-camp and followers."

**Verse 9:** "Similarly others too, equally deluded, came to the sacrifice. Then Dakṣa who was ill disposed towards Śiva received them hospitably."

**Verse 10:** "Large divine mansions of great value and brilliant lustre were erected by Tvaṣṭṛ and assigned to them by Dakṣa."

**Verse 11:** "In all those places they stationed themselves in a befitting manner after being duly honoured. They shone along with Viṣṇu and me."

**Verse 12:** "In that sacrifice that was being performed in that holy place of Kanakhala, Bhṛgu and other sages were made Ṛtviks by him (Dakṣa)."

**Verse 13:** "Viṣṇu himself was the presiding officer along with the Maruts. I was the Brahmā (a special officiating deity) the director and guide for Vedic rituals."

**Verse 14:** "The guardians of the quarters became the gatekeepers and watchmen. They were well-equipped in arms and had many attendants to assist them. They were very enthusiastic."

**Verse 15:** "In that altar, sacrifice itself was present in its beautiful embodied form. The excellent sages became the holders of the Vedas."

**Verse 16:** "The sacrificial fire evinced its diverse forms in a thousand ways, during the sacrificial festivities, in order to receive the sacrificial offerings of Dakṣa."

**Verses 17-18:** "There were eighty-six thousand Ṛtviks in the performance of the sacrifice and sixty-four thousand Udgātṛs. The celestial sages Nārada and others acted as Adhvaryus and Hotṛs. They too were as many. The seven sages (jointly and) severally repeated the Sāman hymns."

**Verse 19:** "In his great sacrifice Dakṣa extended his invitation to Gandharvas, Vidyādharas, Siddhas, Ādityas, all the innumerable Nāgas along with their followers and sacrificial ritualists."

**Verse 20:** "Brahminical, Royal and celestial sages, kings, with their friends, ministers, armies etc, Vasus and other chief Gaṇadevatas—all of them were invited by him in the sacrifice."

**Verse 21:** "With the proper initiation, tying of the holy thread round his wrist and Svastyayana rites duly performed, Dakṣa along with his wife, shone well."

**Verse 22:** "Dakṣa, the evil-minded, did not invite Śiva for that sacrifice, deciding that He was not worthy of taking part in the sacrifice because He was a Kapālin."

**Verse 23:** "In view of the fact that Satī was the wife of Kapālin, she was not invited, though she was his beloved daughter, by Dakṣa who was blind to her qualities."

**Verse 24:** "While the great festivities in the sacrifice of Dakṣa were being celebrated those who had assembled for the same were engrossed in their respective activities."

**Verse 25:** "In the meantime, Dadhīci a devotee of Śiva, realising that lord Śiva was not there became dispirited and spoke thus."

**Dadhīci said:**

**Verse 26:** "O ye all! celestial sages and others, pay heed to my words. Why has not Śiva taken part in the festivities of this sacrifice?"

**Verse 27:** "Of course, the chiefs of devas, the great sages and the guardians of the quarters have all come. Yet the sacrifice cannot be perfect and complete without the noble-souled, trident-holder Śiva."

**Verse 28:** "The bull-bannered, blue-necked eternal Puruṣa the great Īśa is not seen here. Great scholars have affirmed, that all auspicious results happen due to Him alone."

**Verse 29:** "O Dakṣa, if accepted by Triyambaka all inauspicious things become auspicious and fresh auspicious things take shape which will be greater than the greatest in a trice."

**Verse 30:** "Hence the invitation to the great Śiva shall be extended by you yourself immediately or by Brahmā or by Viṣṇu the lord."

**Verse 31:** "By all means Śiva shall be brought here by you along with Indra, the guardians of the quarters, the brahmins and the Siddhas in order to make the sacrifice complete and perfect."

**Verse 32:** "All of you shall go where He is stationed. Immediately bring Śiva along with Satī."

**Verse 33:** "O lords of Devas, everything shall be sanctified through Śiva, the supreme Self. If the consort of Śivā, the great Being, comes here, everything will be all right."

**Verse 34:** "Since all merits accrue from thinking upon Him and repeating His names, the bull-bannered deity shall be brought with all efforts."

**Verse 35:** "If Śiva comes here, the sacrifice will become sanctified, or it will remain incomplete and imperfect. I am telling you the truth."

**Verse 36:** "On hearing his words, the foolish and evil-minded Dakṣa became furious in a trice and said mockingly."

**Dakṣa said:**

**Verse 37:** "Viṣṇu who is the prime cause of all deities and in whom the eternal virtue resides has been invoked here by me. What is it that the sacrificial rite lacks in?"

**Verse 38:** "Viṣṇu in whom all the Vedas, sacrifices and the different rites are founded has graced this place by his presence."

**Verse 39:** "Brahmā, the grandfather of the worlds, has come here from Satyaloka along with the Vedas, Upaniṣads and the Āgamas."

**Verse 40:** "Similarly, the king of devas himself has come along with all the devas. You too, the sages free from sins, have come."

**Verse 41:** "Whoever is worthy of being included in the sacrifice and deserves honour has come. You all know the Vedic texts and their meanings. You all are steady in your rites."

**Verse 42:** "Of what avail is Śiva to us in this place? O brahmin, of course I have given my daughter to Him but that was because I was persuaded by Brahmā."

**Verse 43:** "O brahmin, this Śiva is not a man of nobility. He has neither father nor mother. He is the lord of goblins, ghosts and spirits and is incorrigible."

**Verse 44:** "He is a haughty self-conceited fool with false prestige and hostility. He is unworthy of this sacred rite. Hence he is not invited by me."

**Verse 45:** "Therefore you shall never make such statements as these henceforth. My great sacrifice has to be made fruitful by all of you."

**Brahmā said:**

**Verse 46:** "On hearing those words, Dadhīci spoke aloud within the hearing of devas and sages. His words were pregnant with meaning."

**Dadhīci said:**

**Verse 47:** "This sacrifice has become 'Non-sacrifice' without the presence of Śiva. Indeed your destruction is imminent in this very sacrifice."

**Verse 48:** "Saying this, Dadhīci walked out of the sacrifice of Dakṣa and immediately returned to his hermitage."

**Verse 49:** "Then some important devotees of Śiva who were initiated in the Śaiva cult cursed Dakṣa and returned to their respective abodes."

**Verse 50:** "When the sage Dadhīci and others staged a walkout, the evil-minded Dakṣa, inimical to Śiva, said mocking at them."

**Dakṣa said:**

**Verse 51:** "Gone is that brahmin favourite of Śiva named Dadhīci and others too of his ilk have gone out of my sacrifice."

**Verse 52:** "This has become good. I approve of this always. O Indra, devas and sages, I am telling you the truth."

**Verse 53:** "They are slow-witted and senseless. They are rogues indulging in false deliberations and discussions. They are out of the Vedic circle. These men of evil conduct shall be eschewed from sacrificial rites."

**Verse 54:** "You all, brahmins, sages and devas with Viṣṇu at the head shall make my sacrifice fruitful."

**Brahmā said:**

**Verses 55-56:** "On hearing these words, the celestial sages deluded by Śiva's Māyā performed the worship of the deities in that sacrifice. O great sage, I have thus explained how the sacrifice had been cursed. Now I shall explain how the sacrifice was destroyed."

## Chapter 28 — Satī's Journey (to Dakṣa's Sacrifice)
**URL:** https://www.wisdomlib.org/hinduism/book/shiva-purana-english/d/doc226048.html

**Verses 1-2:** Brahmā describes how Satī was engaged in sports with friends near a fountain on mountain Gandhamādana "when the celestial sages were on their way to Dakṣa's sacrifice."

**Verses 3-4:** Satī observes the moon traveling with Rohiṇī toward the sacrifice and inquires of her attendant about his destination.

**Verse 5:** Satī asks her friend Vijayā: "where does this moon go now in a hurry in the company of Rohiṇī after taking leave of us?"

**Verse 6:** Vijayā approaches the moon to question his whereabouts.

**Verse 7:** The moon informs Vijayā about Dakṣa's sacrificial festival with great respect.

**Verse 8:** Vijayā, greatly disturbed by this news, immediately reports it to goddess Satī.

**Verse 9:** Upon hearing, Satī becomes surprised and ponders the situation.

**Verse 10:** Satī muses: "Dakṣa is my father...Why did they not invite me? Have they forgotten their own beloved daughter?"

**Verse 11:** Satī decides to ask Śiva about the omission.

**Verse 12:** Satī leaves Vijayā behind and goes directly to Śiva.

**Verse 13:** She finds Śiva surrounded by attendants including Nandin in his council-chamber.

**Verse 14:** Satī approaches Śiva quickly to ask him about the matter.

**Verse 15:** Śiva affectionately places his beloved on his lap and delights her with kind words.

**Verse 16:** Śiva, seated among his attendants, addresses Satī while engaged in divine sports.

**Verse 17:** Śiva asks: "O slender-waisted lady, why did you come here in the council-chamber and that too in a state of surprise?"

**Verse 18:** Satī, bowing respectfully with joined palms, responds to Śiva's inquiry.

**Verse 19:** Satī reports: "I have heard that my father is performing a great sacrifice. Great festivities are being conducted there."

**Verse 20:** Satī asks Śiva why he doesn't wish to attend her father's important sacrifice.

**Verse 21:** Satī states: "This is the duty of friends that they shall frequently associate with their friends."

**Verse 22:** Satī requests: "O lord, please come to my father's Sacrificial Hall along with me."

**Verse 23:** Upon hearing Satī's words, Śiva speaks courteously, though wounded by Dakṣa's previous insults.

**Verse 24:** Śiva responds: "Dakṣa is very well your father, dear. But he is my particular enemy."

**Verse 25:** Śiva notes that celestial sages lacking true knowledge now attend Dakṣa's sacrifice.

**Verse 26:** Śiva explains: "those who go to another man's house without being invited attain disrespect which is more serious than even death."

**Verse 27:** Śiva illustrates that even prosperous Indra loses status by visiting uninvited.

**Verse 28:** Śiva concludes: "Hence you and I particularly shall not go to Dakṣa's sacrifice."

**Verse 29:** Śiva compares kinsmen's hurtful words to arrows: "People wounded with arrows by enemies are not so pained as when their vulnerable points are hit."

**Verse 30:** Śiva explains that wicked people fail to recognize when attacking virtuous individuals endowed with noble qualities.

**Verse 31:** Despite Śiva's counsel, Satī becomes angry and responds to him.

**Verse 32:** Satī states: "you by whom sacrifice becomes fruitful have not been invited by my father, thus he has committed a foul deed."

**Verse 33:** Satī desires to understand the thoughts of the "evil-minded" Dakṣa and assembled celestials.

**Verse 34:** Satī requests Śiva's permission to attend her father's sacrifice.

**Verse 35:** Śiva, possessing perfect vision and realizing all, responds to the goddess's request.

**Verse 36:** Śiva grants permission: "if this is what you wish...you can immediately start for your father's sacrifice with my willing permission."

**Verse 37:** Śiva offers: "You can go in royal splendour mounting this bull richly caparisoned."

**Verse 38:** Satī mounts the decorated bull, adorns herself, and departs toward her father's abode.

**Verse 39:** Royal regalia including umbrellas, fans, silken garments, and ornaments accompany her.

**Verse 40:** Sixty thousand of Śiva's attendants enthusiastically accompany Satī with great festivities.

**Verse 41:** The attendants celebrate Satī's departure with magnificent fanfare.

**Verses 42-43:** Heroic attendants sing praises of Śiva and Śivā, jumping joyfully as "the mother of the universe" departs in glorious fashion, filling the three worlds with pleasing sounds.

## Chapter 29 — Satī's Statement (Confrontation with Dakṣa)
**URL:** https://www.wisdomlib.org/hinduism/book/shiva-purana-english/d/doc226049.html

**Verse 1:** Sati reached the sacrificial site where devas, asuras, and sages gathered enthusiastically.

**Verse 2:** She observed her father's mansion filled with wondrous, lustrous things and groups of celestial beings.

**Verse 3:** The Goddess descended from Nandin at the gate and entered the sacrifice site alone.

**Verse 4:** Her mother Asikni and sisters received her respectfully upon seeing her.

**Verse 5:** Daksha showed no affection or respect; others feared him due to Shiva's Maya.

**Verse 6:** Sati bowed to her parents and gazed about in surprise at the cool reception.

**Verse 7:** She observed shares allocated to various deities but none for Shiva, igniting her fury.

**Verse 8:** Slighted and furious, she directed her burning gaze at Daksha and all present.

**Verse 9:** "How can Shiva, who sanctifies the entire universe, not be invited to this sacrifice?"

**Verse 10:** "What sacrifice lacks Shiva, who is sacrifice itself, its performer, fee, and adjunct?"

**Verse 11:** "All rites without Him remain impure; with Him or through remembrance, they become pure."

**Verse 27:** "The arrow which was constituted by Viṣṇu and whose steel head was fire god blazed forth and burnt the three Asuras who lived in the three cities. It thereby removed their sins."

**Verse 28:** "The three cities reduced to ashes fell on the earth girt by the four oceans."

**Verse 29:** "Since they had refrained from the worship of Śiva, hundreds of Asuras were burnt by the fire generated by the arrow. They cried 'Hā Hā' in distress."

**Verse 30:** "Tārakākṣa was burnt along with his two brothers. He remembered his lord Śiva who is favourably disposed to his devotees."

**Verse 31:** "Lamenting in diverse ways and looking up to lord Śiva, he mentally appealed to him."

**Tārakākṣa said:**

**Verse 32:** "'O Śiva, you are known to be pleased with us, if at any future hour you burn us, you will do so along with our kinsfolk. Let it be in accordance with this truth.'"

**Verse 33:** "'What is difficult and inaccessible to the gods and Asuras has been secured by us. Let our intellect be purified by our thoughts on you in every birth.'"

**Verse 34:** "O sage, at the bidding of Śiva, those Asuras were burnt and reduced to ashes by the fire even as they were muttering thus."

**Verse 35:** "Other Asuras too, children and old men were completely burnt out, O Vyāsa, at the bidding of Śiva and speedily reduced to ashes."

**Verse 36:** "Just as the universe is burnt at the end of a Kalpa so also every thing and every one there, whether woman or man or vehicles, was reduced to ashes by that fire."

**Verse 37:** "Some women were forced to leave their husbands necking them and were burnt by the fire. Some were sleeping, some were intoxicated and some were exhausted after their sexual dalliance. All were burnt."

**Verses 38–39:** "Some who were partially burnt woke up and rushed here and there. They fell unconscious and fainted. There was not even a minute particle whether mobile or immobile which escaped unscathed by that terrible Tripura fire excepting Maya, the imperishable Viśvakarman of the Asuras."

**Verse 40:** "Those who were not opposed to the Gods were saved by Śiva's brilliance, those who devoutly sought refuge in lord Śiva at the time of adversity."

**Verse 41:** "Whether Asuras or other beings those whose collective activities were not destructive were saved; others of contrary activities were burnt in fire."

**Verse 42:** "Hence, all possible efforts shall be made by good men to avoid despicable activities whereby people waste away themselves."

**Verse 43:** "Let there be no predicament to any as it happened in regard to the residents of the three cities. This is the opinion of all. By chance if it happens, let it."

**Verse 44:** "Those who worshipped Śiva along with their family attained Gaṇapati's region, thanks to the worship of Śiva."
