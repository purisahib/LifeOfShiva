## Overview
The Linga Purana is one of the eighteen major Mahapuranas, originally composed by Vyasa in 11,000 Sanskrit verses. It covers:
- The Linga symbol of Shiva and its cosmic significance
- Shaiva philosophy and Pashupata tradition
- Cosmology, mythology, astronomy
- Yoga and meditation
- Geography of sacred places and pilgrimage sites
- Ethics and devotional practices

**Structure:** Two sections — Purva-bhaga (108 chapters) and Uttara-bhaga (55 chapters)

**Note from scholars:** Evidence suggests the Uttara-bhaga may be a later addition.

## Key Philosophical Content (from Wikipedia article fetch)

### Linga as Symbol
From the Wikipedia article on Linga Purana (fetched 2026-05-05):
- The Linga is presented as an "aniconic symbol" representing both matter and spirit
- Incorporates Samkhya philosophical concepts
- Discusses how the **signless Brahman manifests as the visible universe**
- The Purana uniquely combines aggressive defense of Shiva against censure with teachings on non-violence
- Ethical principle stated: *"helping everyone and showing kindness to all constitutes supreme worship"*

### Dating and Structure
- Original composition: approximately **5th–10th century CE**
- Two parts: Purva-bhaga (108 chapters) and Uttara-bhaga (55 chapters) — 163 chapters total
- Scholars note the Uttara-bhaga (55 chapters) may be a later addition
