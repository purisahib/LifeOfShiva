## Historical Origins

### Vedic Roots (1500–500 BCE)
Shiva is associated with the Vedic deity **Rudra** — a fearsome storm god mentioned in the Rigveda. Rudra had a dual nature:
- Wild, fierce, destructive (associated with storms and death)
- Gentle, healing, benevolent (Shiva aspect — "auspicious")

The Shri Rudram hymn of the Yajurveda praises both aspects.

### Pre-Vedic Elements
The **Pashupati Seal** from Mohenjo-Daro (Indus Valley Civilization, ~2600–1900 BCE) shows a seated, horned figure surrounded by animals — possibly a proto-Shiva, though scholars debate this.

### Synthesis Period (500 BCE onwards)
The **Shvetashvatara Upanishad** (400–200 BCE) — Rudra-Shiva is identified as:
- The creator and liberator of the cosmos
- The universe's innermost soul
- The one without a second: "Rudra is truly one; for the knowers of Brahman do not admit the existence of a second."

## Position in Hinduism

### In the Trimurti (Holy Trinity)
- **Brahma** = Creator
- **Vishnu** = Preserver/Maintainer
- **Shiva** = Destroyer/Transformer/Dissolver

*Note:* In Shaivism, Shiva performs ALL five functions: **Creation, Preservation, Destruction, Concealment, and Liberation (Grace)**

### In Shaivism
Shiva is the **Supreme Being**, the ultimate reality (Brahman itself). Followers believe:
"Shiva is All and in all — the creator, preserver, destroyer, revealer and concealer."

### In the Smarta Tradition
Shiva appears as one of the **Panchayatana** (five equivalent deities) alongside Vishnu, Devi, Ganesha, and Surya.

### As Adiyogi
The first teacher of yoga, meditation, and inner knowledge. According to Sadhguru's tradition, Shiva transmitted yogic knowledge to his **7 disciples (Saptarishis)** at Lake Kantisarovar, Himalayas.

## Physical Appearance and Iconography

### Body
- Skin: **White** (like camphor or mountain snow) from cremation ashes (vibhuti/bhasma)
- Throat: **Blue** (Nilakantha) from the Halahala poison
- Body: **Smeared with ash** — symbolizing impermanence, renunciation, and the cremation ground
- Form: Both **beautiful and fearsome** simultaneously

### Head
- **Third Eye** on forehead — between the eyebrows — symbolizes omniscience, transcendence, divine fire
- **Crescent Moon** (Shashibhushana) — adorns his hair, symbolizes time cycles, and marks him as Soma (moon lord)
- **Ganges River** — flows from his matted hair (Jata), he captured Ganga to prevent earthly catastrophe
- **Matted Hair (Jata)** — sign of the ascetic, containment of cosmic power
- **Snake Vasuki** — coiled around his neck, representing mastery over serpentine (kundalini) energy, fearlessness of death

### Eyes
- **Three eyes**: Left eye = Moon (emotion, past), Right eye = Sun (intellect, present), Third eye = Fire (wisdom, beyond time)
- The third eye burns ignorance. When opened on Kamadeva — the god of desire was reduced to ashes.

### Clothing and Seat
- **Tiger skin** — represents victory over animal nature and desires
- **Elephant skin** (in some depictions) — conquest of ego
- **Lotus seat** or **Kailash mountain** as throne
- **Deer** in hand — represents the mind (always leaping from one thought to another)

### Weapons and Objects
- **Trishula (Trident)** — represents:
  - The three gunas: Sattva, Rajas, Tamas
  - The three worlds: Heaven, Earth, Underworld
  - The three times: Past, Present, Future
  - Creation, Preservation, Destruction
- **Damaru (Hourglass Drum)** — the primordial sound, AUM, vibration of creation; the Nataraja's drum
- **Parashu (Axe)** — weapon of destruction
- **Pinaka Bow** — given to Janaka by Shiva; the bow Rama broke
- **Pasha (Noose)** — to bind souls
- **Sula (Spear)**

### Mount and Vehicle
- **Nandi the Bull** — sacred vehicle, symbolizes dharma, strength, devotion, joy
- **Nandi** guards Kailash and serves as Shiva's gatekeeper

## Forms and Manifestations

### Panchamukha (Five-Faced) Shiva
The five cosmic aspects:
1. **Sadyojata** (West) — creation, wrathful form, earth element
2. **Vamadeva** (North) — preservation, feminine aspect, water element
3. **Aghora** (South) — destruction/regeneration, fire element, cremation ground
4. **Tatpurusha** (East) — concealment, obscuration, air element
5. **Ishana** (Above/Zenith) — liberation/grace, space element, creative consciousness

### Nataraja — The Cosmic Dancer
The most iconic form — Shiva dancing within a **circle of fire (Prabhamandala)**:
- **Upper right hand**: Damaru (drum) — sound of creation
- **Upper left hand**: Flame — fire of destruction
- **Lower right hand**: Abhaya mudra — "Fear not" — protection
- **Lower left hand**: Pointing to raised left foot — path of liberation
- **Right foot**: Crushing **Apasmara** (the demon of ignorance/forgetfulness)
- **Raised left foot**: Liberation from the cycle of birth and death

The dance is called **Ananda Tandava** (Dance of Bliss) performed in Chidambaram.
A statue of Nataraja stands at the entrance of **CERN** (European nuclear research), recognizing the cosmic symbolism.

### Dakshinamurti
Shiva as the **Universal Teacher** — seated under a banyan tree, facing south, teaching through **silence**. His students are the four **Kumaras** (ancient sages). He transmits wisdom about Vedanta, yoga, music, and the arts.

### Ardhanarishvara
The **half-male, half-female** form — right side is Shiva, left side is Parvati. Represents:
- Unity of masculine and feminine energy
- Purusha (consciousness) and Prakriti (nature)
- Inseparability of Shiva and Shakti

### Bhairava
Shiva's **fierce, terrifying form** — naked, adorned with skulls, accompanied by a dog. Created when Shiva cut off Brahma's fifth head. Represents:
- Annihilation of ego
- Justice
- Guardian of Varanasi and crossroads

**64 forms of Bhairava** exist, 8 main ones called **Ashta Bhairava**.

### Lingam (Shiva Linga)
The most common form of Shiva worship — an aniconic cylindrical stone in a yoni-shaped base representing:
- The formless, infinite nature of Shiva (formless = beyond attributes)
- The axis of the cosmos
- Union of Shiva (consciousness) and Shakti (energy)

**Swayambhu Lingas**: Naturally formed, not installed by humans (e.g., Amarnath ice Shiva Linga)
**Jyotirlingas**: 12 most sacred, where Shiva appeared as light

## Family

### Father/Mother
- Father of the universe: **Paramashiva** (Supreme Shiva, beyond all)
- No birth as such — he is **Svayambhu** (self-existent, uncreated)

### Consorts
1. **Sati** — first wife, daughter of Daksha, self-immolated at Daksha's yajna
2. **Parvati** (Sati reborn) — also known as **Uma, Gauri, Durga, Kali, Ambika, Bhavani**

### Sons
1. **Ganesha** (Ganapati, Vinayaka) — elephant-headed, lord of beginnings, remover of obstacles
2. **Kartikeya** (Skanda, Murugan, Subrahmanya, Kumara) — six-headed war god, general of the divine army

### Daughters (regional/tradition-specific)
1. **Ashokasundari** — born from the wish-fulfilling tree Kalpavriksha when Parvati wished for a daughter
2. **Manasa** — serpent goddess (in some traditions)

### Other Relations
- **Ayyappan** — born of Shiva and Mohini (Vishnu's female form) — worshipped especially in Kerala
- **Ganas** — Shiva's celestial attendants, led by Ganesha as their chief (Ganapati = Lord of the Ganas)
- **Nandi** — his beloved bull devotee and vehicle

## Abode
- **Mount Kailash** — in the Himalayas (Tibet) — his eternal home
- **Varanasi (Kashi)** — his earthly city — he never leaves it
- **Cremation Grounds (Shmashana)** — he meditates in cremation grounds as a sign of transcendence over death

## Philosophical Significance

### Three Pati-Pashu-Pasha
- **Pati**: Shiva as the Lord
- **Pashu**: The individual soul (bound)
- **Pasha**: The bondage (anava/ego, karma, maya)

Shiva's grace removes Pasha, liberating Pashu to realize identity with Pati.

### Shaiva Siddhanta (South India)
Dualistic tradition — Self and Shiva are distinct. Liberation is **proximity to Shiva**, not identity with him.

### Kashmir Shaivism (Trika)
Non-dualistic — seeks **recognition** (pratyabhijna) that one's own consciousness IS Shiva.

### Lingayat Tradition (12th century)
Founded by **Basavanna** — rejected caste hierarchy, emphasized direct devotion to Shiva through the personal linga worn on the body.

## Worship Practices

### Daily Worship Items
- **Bilva (Bel) leaves** — most sacred offering to Shiva; three-lobed leaf = three eyes of Shiva
- **Water** (Jal abhishek) — poured over the Shiva linga
- **Milk** (Dugdha abhishek)
- **Honey** (Madhu abhishek)
- **Curd** (Dadhi abhishek)
- **Ghee** (Sarpi abhishek)
- **Panchamrita** (five nectars)
- **Vibhuti/Bhasma** (sacred ash) — applied to forehead as three horizontal lines (Tripundra)
- **Rudraksha** beads — worn or used as prayer beads

### Sacred Mantras
- **Panchakshara**: **OM NAMAH SHIVAYA** (the five-syllable mantra — Na=earth, Ma=water, Shi=fire, Va=air, Ya=space)
- **Mahamrityunjaya Mantra**: "OM TRYAMBAKAM YAJAMAHE..." (mantra conquering death)
- **Shiva Tandava Stotram**: Composed by Ravana — describes Shiva's cosmic dance
- **Rudram-Chamakam**: Ancient Vedic hymn of 1,000 names

### Sacred Days
- **Mondays** (Somvar) — most auspicious for Shiva worship
- **Pradosh** (13th lunar day, both bright and dark fortnight)
- **Chaturdashi** (14th lunar day)
- **Maha Shivaratri** — "The Great Night of Shiva" — annual festival in February/March

### Maha Shivaratri
Commemorates multiple events (different traditions differ):
- Night of Shiva and Parvati's divine wedding
- Night Shiva performed the cosmic Tandava dance
- Night Shiva swallowed the Halahala poison
- Night the infinite Jyotirlinga first appeared

## Sacred Sites Beyond the 12 Jyotirlingas
- **Amarnath** (Kashmir) — ice Shiva Linga formed naturally each year
- **Pashupatinath** (Kathmandu, Nepal) — most important Shiva temple outside India
- **Kailash Mansarovar** (Tibet) — his mythological abode
- **Brihadishvara Temple** (Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu)
- **Chidambaram Nataraja Temple** (Tamil Nadu) — where Nataraja is the main deity
- **Prambanan** (Indonesia) — Hindu temple complex with Shiva as primary deity
