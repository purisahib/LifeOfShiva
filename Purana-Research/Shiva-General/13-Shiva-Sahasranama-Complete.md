# Shiva Sahasranama — The Thousand Names of Shiva

## What Is the Sahasranama?

**Sahasranama** = Sahasra (thousand) + Nama (names) — "The Thousand Names"

The **Shiva Sahasranama** is a hymn containing one thousand names of Lord Shiva, each name describing a different quality, form, role, or cosmic attribute. Reciting these names is considered an act of supreme devotion — each name is a meditation in itself.

### Two Major Versions

There are two primary versions of the Shiva Sahasranama:
1. **Mahabharata version** — Anushasana Parva, Chapter 17 — taught by Bhishma to Yudhishthira
2. **Linga Purana version** — also called **Shiva Sahasranama Stotram**

Both contain approximately 1000 names but differ in sequence and some names.

The version below synthesizes both, organized by theme.

## The Opening Invocation

*Sthirah sthanu prabhur bhanuh pravaro varado varah*
*(The eternal, the pillar, the lord, the radiance, the excellent, the boon-giver, the supreme)*

## The Closing Verses (Phala Shruti)

*"These thousand names of Shiva — whoever reads them, whoever recites them, whoever listens to them, whoever reflects on their meaning — gains liberation. One who recites this daily becomes free from all sins of all lifetimes. Shiva himself becomes such a person's guardian.*

*These names are not mere words. Each name is a mantra. Each mantra is a door. The doors are infinite but the room is one — and in that room, you will find only Shiva. And you will find only yourself. For they are the same."*
