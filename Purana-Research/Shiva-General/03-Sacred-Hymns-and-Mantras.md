# Sacred Hymns and Mantras of Lord Shiva

---

## 1. PANCHAKSHARA MANTRA — The Five-Syllable Mantra

**OM NAMAH SHIVAYA**
**ॐ नमः शिवाय**

*The most sacred mantra of Shaivism*

### Meaning
- **Om** — the primordial sound, the sound of the universe
- **Na** — Na = Earth element; bowing to the earth-body of Shiva
- **Ma** — Ma = Water element; bowing to the water-body of Shiva
- **Shi** — Shi = Fire element; bowing to the fire-body of Shiva
- **Va** — Va = Air element; bowing to the air-body of Shiva
- **Ya** — Ya = Space/Ether element; bowing to the sky/ether-body of Shiva

Together: *"I bow to Lord Shiva, the auspicious one who pervades all five elements."*

### Significance
- Found in the Krishna Yajurveda (Taittiriya Samhita)
- Also called the **Shiva Panchakshara** (five sacred syllables)
- Reciting this mantra 5 times = equal to worshipping the five-faced (Panchamukha) Shiva
- One of the most ancient and powerful mantras in Hinduism

## 2. MAHAMRITYUNJAYA MANTRA — Victory Over Death

**Full Mantra:**
*Om Tryambakam Yajamahe*
*Sugandhim Pushtivardhanam*
*Urvaarukamiva Bandhanaat*
*Mrityor Mukshiya Maamritaat*

**ॐ त्र्यम्बकं यजामहे सुगन्धिं पुष्टिवर्धनम् ।**
**उर्वारुकमिव बन्धनान् मृत्योर्मुक्षीय माऽमृतात् ।।**

### Meaning — Word by Word
- **Om** — the primordial sound
- **Tryambakam** — the three-eyed one (Shiva)
- **Yajamahe** — we worship, we honor, we adore
- **Sugandhim** — the fragrant one, the sweet-scented one
- **Pushtivardhanam** — the one who nourishes and strengthens all beings
- **Urvaarukamiva** — like the cucumber (which naturally separates from the vine when ripe)
- **Bandhanaat** — from the bondage (of attachment, fear, ego)
- **Mrityor** — from death
- **Mukshiya** — may you liberate me
- **Maa Amritaat** — to the immortal (may I not be separated from immortality)

### Complete Meaning
*"We worship the three-eyed Lord Shiva, who is fragrant and nourishes all living beings. May he liberate us from the bondage of worldly existence and death, just as a ripe cucumber naturally separates from the vine — may we be liberated to immortality."*

### Origin
- Found in **Rigveda 7.59.12** and **Yajurveda 3.60**
- Also called **Mrityunjaya Mantra**, **Rudra Mantra**, and **Trayambaka Mantra**
- Addressed to **Tryambaka** — the three-eyed one = Rudra/Shiva

### Benefits (Traditional)
- Liberation from cycle of birth and death (Moksha)
- Protection from all diseases
- Healing of terminal illnesses
- Removal of fear of death
- Spiritual liberation

## 3. SHRI RUDRAM — The Vedic Hymn

The most ancient and most sacred hymn to Rudra-Shiva, from the **Krishna Yajurveda** (Taittiriya Samhita, 4.5).

### Structure
Two sections:
1. **Namakam** (Chapter 5 of Kanda 4) — The Names of Rudra
2. **Chamakam** (Chapter 7 of Kanda 4) — The Requests

Each section has **11 anuvaka** (stanzas/hymns).

### Namakam — Key Content
The Namakam addresses Rudra's dual nature:
- His **fierce aspect** (Rudra — the terrible, the destroyer)
- His **benevolent aspect** (Shiva — the auspicious, the healer)

The devotee prays: *"May the fierce Rudra withdraw his destructive arrows. May his auspicious, gentle form (Shiva) be present with us."*

Key verses from Namakam:
- *"Namas te Rudra manyave"* — "Salutations to your anger, O Rudra"
- Shiva is present in the marketplace, in armies, in forests, in every being
- He is present in craftsmen, in the blacksmith, in the healer
- He is present in every path, every direction, every element
- **The fundamental teaching: Shiva is EVERYWHERE. There is no place he is not.**

### Chamakam — What is Requested
The devotee asks Shiva to grant:
- Long life and health
- Progeny and prosperity
- Victory over enemies
- Knowledge and wisdom
- Food and sustenance
- Freedom from disease
- Spiritual liberation

*Ultimately: "Grant me everything needed for a complete, dharmic life, and finally — liberation."*

### Significance
- Chanted daily in all major Shiva temples across India
- The **Maha Rudra** ceremony = reciting the Shri Rudram 11 times
- The **Ati-Rudra** ceremony = reciting it 14,641 times (11 × 11 × 11 × 11) over 12 days

## 4. SHIVA TANDAVA STOTRAM — Hymn of the Cosmic Dance

*Composed by Ravana, demon king of Lanka, while trapped under Mount Kailash*

### The Story of Its Composition
Ravana was flying back from conquering Kubera's city in his Pushpaka Vimana when he tried to move Mount Kailash (Shiva's abode). Shiva simply pressed his toe — Kailash became immovable, trapping Ravana beneath it.

In agony but determined, Ravana cut off his own heads one by one and fashioned them into musical instruments. He played and sang this hymn for **a thousand years** while trapped under the mountain.

Finally, Shiva, overcome by the devotion, lifted his toe and freed Ravana — and gave him the Chandrahasa sword as a reward.

### All 15 Verses — Summary

**Verse 1 (Jatavitavigalaj-Jala-Pravaha):**
*"With water flowing and dripping from his coiled forest of matted hair, upon his neck that is purified by the waters, with snakes hanging like garlands, with the Damaru drum that emits the sound Damat Damat Damat Damat — Lord Shiva performs the auspicious Tandava dance."*

**Verse 2 (Jatakatahasambhrama):**
*"His matted hair whirls like a caldron with the Ganges roaring within; his third eye blazes on his forehead while the crescent moon shines — Shiva performs the Tandava dance."*

**Verse 3 (Dharadharendra-Nandini):**
*"Accompanied by the Divine Mother (Parvati/daughter of the mountain), with the horizon shaking from the force of his dance — waves of joy arise."*

**Verse 4 (Jata-Bhujanga-Pinggala):**
*"The reddish-brown serpents on his matted hair throb with raised hoods, their lustre smearing the sky with saffron."*

**Verse 5 (Sahasra-Locana-Prabhrity):**
*"His dancing feet produce dust that graces celestial beings; his crown is the moon; his body is adorned with serpent garlands."*

**Verse 6 (Lalaatta-Catvara-Jvalad):**
*"Upon the surface of his forehead burns a spark of divine fire that destroyed Kama (desire). The moon shines above while Shiva grants liberation."*

**Verse 7 (Karaala-Bhaala-Pattika):**
*"His fearsome forehead burns with the fire that consumed Kama. His footsteps paint creation. He is the sole artist of the universe."*

**Verse 8 (Naviina-Megha-Manddali):**
*"His dance restrains the unrestrainable cosmic forces. He wears the Ganga and elephant hide. He extends boundless auspiciousness."*

**Verse 9 (Praphulla-Nila-Pankaja):**
*"Worship to the Destroyer of Smara (Kama), Destroyer of the three cities (Tripura), Destroyer of all delusion, all demons."*

**Verse 10 (Akharva-Sarva-Manggalaa):**
*"He is the inexhaustible source of all welfare. From him, all arts blossom. Nectar of eternal joy surges from his dance."*

**Verse 11 (Jayat-Vad-Abhravibhrama):**
*"His eyebrows move with mastery. Serpents hiss on his body. The mridangam drum beats the rhythm of his passionate dance."*

**Verse 12 (Drssad-Vicitra-Talpayoh) — Prayer for Equanimity:**
*"When will I achieve equal vision — seeing the same in comfort and discomfort, in gems and clay, in friends and enemies — and worship Sadashiva?"*

**Verse 13 (Kadaa Nilimpa-Nirjharii) — Prayer for Renunciation:**
*"When will I dwell in forest caves by the Ganges, freed from sinful thoughts, with hands raised and eyes closed, chanting Shiva's holy name?"*

**Verse 14 (Imam Hi Nityam) — The Promise:**
*"Those who regularly recite this hymn with purity of mind will advance toward Shiva. Meditation on Shankara destroys all delusion."*

**Verse 15 (Puuja-Avasaana-Samaye) — The Blessing:**
*"Those who recite this hymn of the ten-headed Ravana after worship of Shiva will receive prosperity and the gracious blessings of Lakshmi from Shambhu."*

## 5. SHIVA PANCHAKSHARA STOTRAM — Five-Verse Hymn

*Composed by Adi Shankaracharya*

Five verses, each focusing on one syllable of NAMAH SHIVAYA:

**Na (Earth):**
*"Nagendraharaya Trilochanaya... Salutations to Shiva who wears the serpent king as garland..."*

**Ma (Water):**
*"Mandakini-Salila-Chandana-Charchitaya... To Shiva anointed with the waters of the Mandakini..."*

**Shi (Fire):**
*"Shiva Bhaktanam Ashritaya... To Shiva who is the refuge of his devotees..."*

**Va (Air):**
*"Vasishtha Kumbhoda-Bhava Gautamadyaih... Worshipped by sages Vasishtha, Agastya, Gautama..."*

**Ya (Space):**
*"Yaksha Svarupa-Ya... To Shiva who is the form of the Yakshas..."*

## 7. OM NAMAH SHIVAYA — Deeper Esoteric Meaning

The five-syllable mantra maps to the entire cosmos:

| Syllable | Element | World | Body Part | Goddess |
|----------|---------|-------|-----------|---------|
| Na | Earth | Bhuloka | Legs | Brahmi |
| Ma | Water | Bhuvarloka | Hands | Maheshvari |
| Shi | Fire | Svarloka | Shoulders | Kaumari |
| Va | Air | Maharloka | Mouth | Vaishnavi |
| Ya | Space/Ether | Janaloka | Third Eye | Indrani |

*Each time the mantra is recited, the entire five-element cosmos is brought to consciousness.*
