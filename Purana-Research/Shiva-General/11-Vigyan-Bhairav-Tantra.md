# Vigyan Bhairav Tantra — The 112 Meditation Techniques of Shiva

## What Is It?

The **Vigyan Bhairav Tantra** (Vijnanabhairava Tantra) is one of the most important texts of **Kashmir Shaivism** and arguably the most comprehensive manual of meditation ever compiled.

- **Full name:** Vijnanabhairava Tantra (Sanskrit: विज्ञानभैरव तन्त्र)
- **Meaning:** "The divine consciousness (Vijnana) of Bhairava (Shiva)" — or "The techniques for recognizing the consciousness of Bhairava"
- **Structure:** 163 verses — a dialogue between Shiva (Bhairava) and his Shakti (Bhairavi/Parvati)
- **Language:** Sanskrit
- **Dating:** Estimated 7th–9th century CE; part of the Rudrayamala Tantra tradition
- **English translations:** Paul Reps summarized them in "Zen Flesh, Zen Bones"; Swami Lakshman Joo gave commentary; Osho discussed all 112 in depth

---

## The Format

Parvati (as Bhairavi) asks Shiva:
*"O Shiva, what is your reality? What is this wonder-filled universe? What constitutes seed? Who centers the universal wheel? What is this life beyond form pervading forms? How may we enter it fully, above space and time, names and descriptions? Let my doubts be cleared."*

Shiva responds with 112 dharanas (meditation techniques) — each a different door to the same destination: recognition of pure consciousness, which IS Shiva.

## The 112 Meditation Techniques — Complete Categorized List

### CATEGORY 1: BREATH AWARENESS (Techniques 1–9)

**1. The gap between inbreath and outbreath**
Focus on the moment the inbreath ends and before the outbreath begins. In that gap — there is no breath. There is only awareness. That awareness is Shiva.

**2. The gap between outbreath and inbreath**
Same — focus on the moment the outbreath ends and before the inbreath begins. Rest in that gap.

**3. Breathe into the heart**
Feel the breath entering the heart center (Anahata), not just the lungs. As breath rises and falls in the heart, feel the consciousness that witnesses it.

**4. The breath as two birds**
Visualize the inbreath and outbreath as two birds flying out from and returning to a center point. You are the center.

**5. Breath fills the body**
Feel the breath as if it fills every cell. As the inbreath spreads everywhere and the outbreath takes everything away — be the awareness of that spreading and withdrawing.

**6. Merge with breath**
Identify completely with the movement of breath — become the breath itself. When you stop separating yourself from breath, you realize you are the consciousness in which breath moves.

**7. Emptying**
As the outbreath leaves, let everything go with it. Empty the lungs completely. In that moment of complete emptiness — who is aware?

**8. The cosmic breath**
Extend your awareness: the universe breathes too — stars expand and contract, oceans rise and fall. Feel yourself breathing with the universe.

**9. Count the exhalations**
Count each outbreath from 1 to 100. This trains one-pointed concentration — the prerequisite for all deeper meditation.

### CATEGORY 2: CENTER AND CONSCIOUSNESS (Techniques 10–22)

**10. The center of your being**
Close your eyes. Find the center of your body. Not the physical center — the felt center of your existence. Rest there.

**11. Spin to the center**
Spin in circles until dizzy. When you stop, the world still spins. In that spinning, you can feel a center that is NOT spinning. That is you.

**12. Lie down and feel bodyless**
Lie completely still. Feel the weight of the body dropping away. After some time, forget you have a body. What remains?

**13. Watch your thoughts like clouds**
Let thoughts come and go. Do not follow any thought. Watch them like clouds — they arise, drift, disappear. You are the sky.

**14. Feel your whole body at once**
Rather than moving attention from part to part — try to feel the entire body simultaneously. This activates the witness consciousness.

**15. Focus on any one spot**
Choose one point on the body — a fingertip, the tip of the tongue, the middle of the forehead. Hold attention there without wavering. Eventually the point disappears and only awareness remains.

**16. The third eye point**
Focus between the eyebrows (the Ajna chakra). Not straining — just gently resting awareness there. This is the point Shiva's third eye opens from.

**17. The hollow in the skull**
Imagine the inside of the skull as a hollow cave. Rest awareness in that inner space.

**18. Both eyes at once toward the nose**
Let both eyes converge gently toward the tip of the nose. This creates a unique state of visual withdrawal that draws awareness inward.

**19. The back of the head**
Rest awareness at the back of the skull. This is the point where the spine meets the brain — the gateway between physical and subtle.

**20. Feel the void in the heart**
In the center of the chest — feel an open, hollow space. Not the physical heart — the empty space around it. Rest in that space.

**21. Above thought**
Look at the gap between two thoughts. That gap is empty — nothing is happening. What is aware of that gap?

**22. Feel the universe as your body**
Expand your body-awareness outward. The skin ends — but awareness doesn't. The room is your body. The building is your body. The earth is your body. The universe is your body.

### CATEGORY 3: VISION AND LIGHT (Techniques 23–32)

**23. Stare at darkness**
Sit in complete darkness. Stare into the blackness. The eyes relax. Eventually, inner light appears — this is the light of pure consciousness.

**24. Look at a point of light**
Fix your gaze on a single flame or point of light. Do not blink. When the eyes water and the gaze dissolves — what remains?

**25. Close your eyes and see the inner light**
After gazing at a bright object, close the eyes. The afterimage appears. Follow it inward as it fades — into pure light consciousness.

**26. Shiva's infinite forms**
Contemplate that Shiva takes infinite forms. Each person is a form of Shiva. Each tree. Each stone. Feel the same consciousness in all of them.

**27. See everything as a dream**
All experiences are like dreams — vivid but passing. If waking experience is a dream, then who is dreaming? That witness is Shiva.

**28. The formless**
Every form you have ever known — has appeared and disappeared. What has remained constant through all changes? That is Brahman/Shiva — the formless.

**29. Space between objects**
Look at the space between objects — not at the objects themselves. The objects are forms; the space is formless. Resting in space, you rest in the formless.

**30. Dissolve a color into its source**
Stare at any color. Watch as it deepens into light. Light deepens into pure brilliance. Pure brilliance fades into pure awareness. 

**31. Contemplate the sky**
Look at a clear open sky. Let the gaze soften until sky and awareness merge. The infinite sky and infinite consciousness are one.

**32. See with half-closed eyes**
Half-close the eyes so that only a sliver of light enters. This partial withdrawal technique creates a unique threshold state between outer and inner.

### CATEGORY 4: SOUND AND MANTRA (Techniques 33–41)

**33. OM as the bridge**
Chant AUM slowly. Let the A fill the belly, U fill the chest, M fill the head. Then feel the silence after M. That silence is Shiva.

**34. The sound that is everywhere**
Listen. Not to specific sounds — listen to the ambient sound underneath all sounds. That ever-present sound is the Nada Brahman.

**35. Block the ears and listen**
Press your palms over both ears firmly. Listen to the internal sound — a humming or ringing. Follow it inward until it becomes a finer and finer sound, and then silence.

**36. Resonate with a sound**
Chant any note or sound. Feel how it resonates in the body. When the sound ends — the vibration remains briefly. Stay with that fading resonance until it fades into silence. Then stay with the silence.

**37. Listen to music in silence**
After music ends, stay in the silence that follows. That silence is more filled with music than the music was.

**38. The sound of your name**
When someone calls your name — in that fraction of a second before you respond — who hears? Not the mind with its identity — just awareness. That is you.

**39. AH as the cosmic sound**
The sound "AH" requires no movement of tongue or palate — pure open sound. It is the first sound of the alphabet in Sanskrit. Rest in the openness of AH.

**40. The mantra as vibration not meaning**
Repeat any mantra — not for meaning but for vibration. Feel how the vibration fills your body. Eventually the vibration replaces thought.

**41. Silence between mantras**
In repeating a mantra, the silence between repetitions is as important as the mantra itself. Rest in those silences.

### CATEGORY 5: BODY AND SENSATION (Techniques 42–61)

**42. The touch of softness**
When you feel something very soft — a fabric, a gentle breeze — let that softness spread inward. Become soft inside.

**43. The pain as witness**
When pain arises in the body — do not fight it or run from it. Watch it the way you watch a cloud. Who is watching the pain?

**44. Bliss in the body**
Remember the last time you felt great joy or bliss. Feel it again — not the memory, the actual sensation. Where is it in the body? Rest in that sensation.

**45. Sexual energy as Shakti**
At the peak of sexual excitement, withdraw the attention from the external object and bring it within. Feel the energy as pure Shakti (creative power). Rest in that energy without outlet — it transforms.

**46. Before sleep**
In the moments before sleep, as the body relaxes but the mind is still aware — rest in that threshold. This is a natural Samadhi state.

**47. Waking from sleep**
In the moments of waking from sleep — before thought begins — there is a pure awareness. Catch that moment. That is Shiva.

**48. The moment of orgasm/sneeze/fright**
At any moment of sudden, involuntary release (orgasm, sneeze, startlement, cough) — the mind goes blank for a fraction of a second. That blank moment is pure consciousness. Extend that moment.

**49. Tickling**
When being tickled and laughing uncontrollably — in that complete helplessness — who is experiencing this? That witness is always free.

**50. Feel the joy of reunion**
When you meet a dear friend after long separation — that sudden surge of joy before words begin — rest in that joy in its purest form.

**51. Contemplating infinite space**
Feel the space inside your chest, your skull, your belly. Expand it outward. The space inside and outside merge. That infinite space is Shiva.

**52. Contemplate atomic smallness**
Feel your body as a collection of atoms. Those atoms are mostly empty space. Contemplate that you are mostly nothingness. What remains when the matter dissolves?

**53. Contemplate cosmic vastness**
Feel your tiny body against the vastness of the sky, the earth, the cosmos. Let the self shrink to nothing in that vastness. What remains?

**54. In a moving vehicle**
While traveling in a moving vehicle, feel only the movement. Not the vehicle, not the destination — just the pure sensation of movement through space.

**55. Look at a clear sky while lying down**
Lie on your back and stare up at the open sky. Let the boundary between your body and the sky dissolve. 

**56. Food as grace**
While eating, feel each bite as Shiva's gift entering you. The food becomes your body. Your body IS Shiva's gift.

**57. Any intense experience**
In any intense experience — fear, joy, anger, grief — there is a moment before the mind labels it where the experience is pure energy. Catch that moment.

**58. Contemplate the world as picture**
See everything around you as a painted picture — beautiful but flat, not three-dimensional. Everything becomes a painting. Who is looking at the painting?

**59. Feel gravity**
Feel the weight of your body being pulled toward the earth. That pull is the earth's love. Rest in that feeling of being held.

**60. The moment before crying**
When you are about to cry, feel the sensation in the chest and throat before tears come. Stay with that sensation. It is pure emotion before the story.

**61. Rest in complete exhaustion**
After great physical effort, lie down completely exhausted. In that collapse, there is a moment when the separate self also collapses. Rest there.

### CATEGORY 6: IMAGINATION AND VISUALIZATION (Techniques 62–80)

**62. Imagine you have no body**
Sit quietly and imagine the body has become transparent, then invisible. There is only awareness — floating, bodyless.

**63. Imagine infinite distance**
Feel the distance between yourself and the most distant star — billions of light years. Feel that same infinite distance within yourself.

**64. Imagine you never existed**
Contemplate that you were never born. There is no "you" to have problems, desires, or fears. What remains when "you" disappear?

**65. This world as illusion**
Look around you. Contemplate that everything you see may be an illusion — like a dream. If it is, then what is real?

**66. Feel everything is light**
Contemplate that everything — walls, trees, people, your own body — is made of condensed light. Everything is light, including you.

**67. Imagine Shiva's presence**
Visualize Shiva standing before you. Feel his presence. Then feel his presence entering you. Then feel that you ARE Shiva.

**68. Visualize your guru's face**
Visualize the face of your spiritual teacher in perfect detail. Let it become radiant with inner light. Merge with that light.

**69. Any beautiful object**
Look at something beautiful — a flower, a flame, a beloved face. Let the beauty enter you. Rest in the aesthetic experience (Rasa).

**70. Contemplate the moment of creation**
Imagine the universe before creation — complete void. Then the first vibration. Follow that vibration as it expands into all of creation.

**71. Meditate on your own death**
Calmly contemplate your own death — the body lying still, all activity ceased. Who is contemplating this? That witness is immortal.

**72. Feel that you will never die**
Contemplate the part of you that was never born and will never die — the witnessing consciousness. Rest in that.

**73. The universe in the skull**
Contemplate the inside of the skull as containing the universe. Everything that exists is inside this skull of consciousness.

**74. See the universe in the heart**
Feel the heart as containing the entire cosmos. Every star, every being, every event — all within the heart.

**75. Feel the emptiness of space**
The universe is mostly empty space. Contemplate that this space is not empty — it is filled with Shiva's consciousness.

**76. Think of nothing**
Try to think of absolutely nothing — no image, no concept, no word, no feeling. In the effort itself, the witness becomes clear.

**77. Be the sky**
Don't have a body — BE the sky. Everything arises within you the way clouds arise in the sky.

**78. Imagine the universe as your body**
Every atom in the universe is part of your body. The sun is your heart. The rivers are your blood. The mountains are your bones.

**79. See no difference anywhere**
Look around and see only one substance — consciousness — appearing as different forms. Not intellectually — actually FEEL it.

**80. Everything is Brahman**
Whatever you see, hear, taste, touch, or think — rest in the recognition that it is all Brahman/Shiva. Even this technique is Shiva.

### CATEGORY 7: AWARENESS OF AWARENESS (Techniques 81–112)

**81. Who am I?**
Ask: "Who am I?" Don't answer with words. Feel the question. Rest in the silence that follows.

**82. What is in the middle of the world?**
Everything has a middle — an atom has a nucleus, a person has a spine, the earth has a core. What is in the middle of consciousness itself?

**83. Be the witness**
Whatever is happening — joy, anger, boredom, pain — simply be the witness of it. Not the experiencer — the witness.

**84. The present moment only**
Past is memory. Future is imagination. Only the present is real. Rest completely in this present moment.

**85. The space between thoughts**
Observe the space between two thoughts. That space is not nothing — it is pure consciousness.

**86. Find the thinker**
When a thought arises, ask: "Who is thinking this?" Not with another thought — but by turning awareness back on itself.

**87. The witness of the witness**
You are aware of your thoughts. Now be aware of the awareness itself. The consciousness that witnesses — who watches it?

**88. Nowhere to go**
There is nowhere to go, nothing to achieve, nothing to become. You are already what you are seeking. Rest in this.

**89. There is nothing outside**
Close your eyes. Feel that everything external has dissolved. There is nothing outside. What is inside?

**90. I am this moment**
You are not who you were yesterday or who you will be tomorrow. Right now, in this exact moment — be completely THIS.

**91. Consciousness without content**
Usually consciousness has an object — you are conscious OF something. Try to have consciousness WITHOUT any object. Pure awareness.

**92. The observer is the observed**
The division between you and what you observe is false. The observer IS the observed. Feel this non-separation.

**93. The infinite cannot be known**
The mind can only know finite things. The Infinite (Shiva) cannot be grasped by mind — only recognized when the mind goes quiet.

**94. Rest in not-knowing**
Instead of trying to know — rest in not knowing. In that space of complete openness, something reveals itself.

**95. I am not the body**
The body changes — grows, ages, sickens, dies. But there is something that watches this that does not change. That is you.

**96. I am not the mind**
The mind also changes — moods, memories, thoughts come and go. The witness of the mind does not change. That is you.

**97. I am not the ego**
Even the sense of "I" that labels all experience — this comes and goes. In deep sleep, the ego is gone. What remains? That is you.

**98. Pure being**
Not being ANYTHING in particular. Just pure being — existence without quality, without identity, without direction.

**99. Bliss is your nature**
You don't need to seek bliss — bliss is your fundamental nature. Every moment of happiness is a brief recognition of what you already are.

**100. Consciousness pervades all**
The same consciousness that is reading these words is the consciousness in the stone, the tree, the animal, the enemy. It is one.

**101. The universe is in you**
Not that you are a small part of the universe — the universe is a small part of you. Consciousness contains the universe, not the reverse.

**102. Let there be no separation**
There is no inside and outside. The sky you see is inside your consciousness. The thoughts are outside — observable. There is no wall.

**103. Pure presence**
Right now, in this moment — without any technique, without any seeking — what is? Just this. Just this presence. This is Shiva.

**104. Nothing to do**
Nothing needs to be done. Nothing needs to be achieved. There is nowhere to arrive. You are already home.

**105. Be without thought for one moment**
For just one moment — no thought, no feeling, no image. Just one moment of complete silence. In that moment, Shiva.

**106. Before you were born**
What were you before you were born? Not philosophically — feel it. That pre-birth state is still present as the deepest layer of you.

**107. After you die**
What will you be after the body dies and the mind dissolves? That state — which has no form, no memory, no thought — is already present now, underneath everything.

**108. You cannot know it**
The final truth cannot be known by the mind, spoken by the tongue, or grasped by the intellect. It can only be. BE.

**109. The teaching is complete**
All 108 techniques are 108 different doors to the same room. If even one door opens for you — you have arrived.

**110. Shiva and Shakti as one**
This entire dialogue — Bhairavi asking, Bhairava answering — is itself a meditation. They are one. The question and the answer are one. The seeker and the sought are one.

**111. Grace**
After all effort, all technique — there comes a moment when you cannot go further by your own effort. In that surrender, grace descends. Shiva comes to the devoted.

**112. I am Shiva**
After all the techniques, after all the searching — this recognition: **I am Shiva. I always was. There was never any separation.**

## Why the Vigyan Bhairav Tantra Is Extraordinary

1. **Non-religious** — No temple required, no priest, no ritual. Pure consciousness practice.
2. **Universal** — The techniques work regardless of belief, culture, or background
3. **Complete** — 112 techniques cover every possible entry point into consciousness
4. **Practical** — Each technique is specific and actionable, not abstract
5. **Dialogic** — Parvati's questions represent every sincere seeker; Shiva's answers are direct

The text influenced not just Indian spirituality but all of world meditation — it predates Zen, Sufi, and Christian mystical traditions and contains techniques that independently appeared in all of them.
