# Lord Shiva in the Ramayana

## Overview

The **Ramayana** of Valmiki (composed c. 500–200 BCE) is primarily the story of **Rama** (Vishnu's avatar). Yet Shiva appears throughout the epic as a powerful, respected, and central figure. The connections between Shiva and Rama are so deep that one of the 12 Jyotirlingas — **Rameshwaram** — was established by Rama himself.

---

## 1. THE PINAKA BOW — SHIVA'S BOW BROKEN BY RAMA

### What is the Pinaka?

The **Pinaka** (also called Shiva Dhanu, Shiva's Bow) was the divine bow of Lord Shiva — one of the most powerful weapons in existence. It was said that the sound of its string being drawn could shake the three worlds.

When Shiva and Vishnu once had a mild celestial dispute to determine who was more powerful, Brahma and other gods arranged a test of their bows. The contest ended inconclusively but resulted in **Shiva's bow being deposited** with the royal family of Videha (Janaka's lineage).

### The Swayamvar of Sita

**King Janaka** of Mithila possessed the Pinaka. He had placed it as the condition for his daughter **Sita's** swayamvar (self-choice marriage ceremony):

*"She who marries my daughter must first lift and string the Pinaka — the bow of Lord Shiva."*

Hundreds of kings and princes had tried. No one could even lift it. It sat in an eight-wheeled chariot requiring 5000 men to move.

**Rama**, at the request of sage Vishwamitra, approached the bow. He:
1. Lifted it with one hand
2. Strung it
3. And when he pulled the string — the bow **broke in two** with a sound like a thunderclap

The earth trembled. The sound was heard across all three worlds.

### Shiva and Rama — The Exchange

When Parashurama heard that Shiva's bow had been broken, he arrived furious:

*"Who broke the Shiva Dhanu? No ordinary mortal can do this. Show me your face."*

Parashurama carried his own divine bow — also gifted by Shiva (the Vishnu Dhanu, the bow of Vishnu).

When Parashurama faced Rama, Rama picked up Parashurama's bow also — the Vishnu Dhanu:

*"If your bow is strung, I will fight you. If not, you must acknowledge that I am superior."*

Parashurama, who recognized that Rama was Vishnu in human form, surrendered. He acknowledged that Rama had fulfilled the purpose of breaking Shiva's bow — it was the cosmic order reasserting itself.

**Significance:** Both the Shiva Dhanu (Pinaka) and Vishnu Dhanu were divine weapons. When Rama (Vishnu) broke Shiva's bow, it symbolized the merging of Shaiva and Vaishnava power streams in the avatar.

## 2. RAVANA AS SHIVA'S GREATEST DEVOTEE

### Ravana's Devotion to Shiva

**Ravana** — the ten-headed demon king of Lanka — is one of the most famous devotees of Shiva in all of Hindu tradition. The Ramayana, while casting him as the villain, consistently acknowledges his extraordinary devotion to Shiva.

### The Mount Kailash Incident

When Ravana was flying over the Himalayas in his Pushpaka Vimana, he saw Mount Kailash blocking his path.

Nandi (Shiva's bull companion and doorkeeper) blocked him and warned him away. Ravana, arrogant, insulted Nandi — calling him monkey-faced.

Nandi cursed him: *"Your kingdom will be destroyed by monkeys."*

(This curse was the foreshadowing of the monkey army of Hanuman and Sugriva destroying Lanka.)

Then Ravana decided to show his strength. He placed his twenty hands under Mount Kailash and began to lift it.

Kailash shook. Parvati was frightened and clung to Shiva.

**Shiva simply pressed his toe down on the mountain.**

Kailash became immovable as stone. Ravana's arms were pinned under the mountain. He screamed in agony.

### The Shiva Tandava Stotram

Ravana, trapped and in agony, performed an act of supreme devotion:

He cut off his own **ten heads** one by one and fashioned them into a **veena** (string instrument), using his own nerves and sinews as strings. He played devotional music and sang the **Shiva Tandava Stotram** — a hymn of 15 verses describing Shiva's cosmic dance.

*Opening verse:*
*"Jatatavigalajjala pravahapavitasthale, Gale avalambya lambitam bhujangatungamalikam — Damad damad damad damad ninesvanadavaddamam, Chakara chandtandavam tanotu nah shivah shivam"*

*("With the flow of water from his matted hair washing his neck clean, wearing a garland of the great serpent on his throat — as the Damaru drum beats Damat Damat — Shiva performed the divine Tandava dance. May that Shiva bestow auspiciousness on us.")*

For **thousands of years** Ravana played and sang in agony.

**Shiva was moved.** He released his toe. He freed Ravana. As a gift for this supreme devotion under suffering, Shiva gave Ravana:
- The **Chandrahas sword** — one of the most powerful weapons
- Renewed strength
- His personal blessings

*Shiva renamed Ravana at this moment:* The original name was Dasagriva (ten-necked). *"Because you cried in your devotion — you are also Ravana (the one who makes the universe cry in awe and devotion)."*

### Ravana's Other Shiva Devotion

The Ramayana references Ravana performing the **Shiva Stava** (hymns to Shiva) daily. Even on the day of his death, during the battle with Rama, Ravana's daily worship of Shiva was unbroken.

This creates one of the most complex figures in Hindu literature: a great sinner, a great devotee, simultaneously.

*Valmiki presents this without judgment — as if to say: Shiva's devotion is independent of one's moral choices, and both can coexist in the same being.*

## 3. THE RAMESHWARAM JYOTIRLINGA — ESTABLISHED BY RAMA

### The Brahmahatya Problem

After killing **Ravana** (who was a Brahmin — a descendant of the sage Pulastya), **Rama** had committed **Brahmahatya** (the sin of killing a Brahmin). Even though the killing was just (Ravana was evil), the sin attached to Rama by cosmic law.

### Rama's Decision

Rama decided to expiate this sin by installing a Shiva Linga at **Rameswaram** (the southern tip of India) and worshipping Shiva there.

### The Divine Linga

Rama sent **Hanuman** north to the Himalayas to bring a Shiva Linga for the puja. Hanuman was the fastest and most powerful — he was the right choice.

But Hanuman took longer than expected. The auspicious moment (muhurta) for the puja was approaching.

**Sita** quickly fashioned a Shiva Linga from the **sand of the seashore** and gave it to Rama.

Rama performed the puja with the sand Linga.

When Hanuman arrived with the mountain Linga from the Himalayas, he was upset — why hadn't they waited?

Rama said: *"Install the Linga you brought beside the one Sita made. They shall both be worshipped here forever. But the sand Linga, made by Sita's hands, shall be primary."*

Hanuman worried that people would prioritize the stone Linga over the sand Linga. He wrapped his tail around the stone Linga and tried to move it — it wouldn't move. Such was the power of Sita's faith that had entered the sand.

**Shiva appeared and declared:**
*"The Linga installed by Rama shall be known as RAMANATHASWAMY — Lord Rama's lord (Shiva). This Linga shall be one of my 12 Jyotirlingas. It shall purify all who visit it."*

The **Ramanathaswamy Temple** at Rameswaram stands there to this day — one of the most sacred Shiva temples in India.

### Why This Story Is Profound

- **Rama (Vishnu's avatar) worshipped Shiva** — the ultimate declaration that Shaiva and Vaishnava paths lead to the same truth
- Shiva received worship from Vishnu himself — completing the circle of cosmic devotion
- **The Brahmahatya expiation** shows that Shiva is the ultimate cleanser — even the worst sins dissolve in his worship

## 4. HANUMAN — SHIVA'S SON AS RAMA'S DEVOTEE

**Hanuman** is considered one of Shiva's avatars (or partial incarnations) in several traditions. He is also Rama's most devoted servant.

This creates a theological harmony:
- Hanuman = Shiva's energy manifested
- Rama = Vishnu manifested
- Hanuman serving Rama = Shiva serving Vishnu = complete cosmic union

The Tamil tradition (Shaiva Siddhanta) teaches:
*"When you see Hanuman worshipping Rama, you are seeing Shiva worshipping Vishnu — the two great cosmic principles dancing together in service of dharma."*

---

## 5. THE UTTARA RAMAYANA — SHIVA'S BLESSINGS AT THE END

The **Uttara Kanda** (later addition to Ramayana) contains a scene where Shiva appears to Rama near the end of his reign:

*"Shiva appeared and said: 'Rama, your work on earth is complete. The bridge of dharma has been repaired. Lanka has been purified. The cosmic purpose of your avatar is fulfilled. When you choose to leave this earth — I will be there.'"*

This is understood as Shiva's promise: at the moment of Rama's return to his divine state, Shiva was present — as he is at all moments of final liberation.

---

## 6. AGASTYA'S ROLE — SHIVA'S SAGE IN RAMAYANA

Sage **Agastya** appears in the Ramayana as a key figure who aids Rama. He was a devotee of both Shiva and Vishnu.

Agastya gave Rama the **Brahmastra** and the **Aditya Hridayam** (Sun hymn). He also told Rama about the Ramanathaswamy pilgrimage.

The Ramayana thus shows Shiva devotees as natural allies of Vishnu's purpose — not competitors.

## Summary: Shiva's Presence in the Ramayana

| Episode | What Happens |
|---------|-------------|
| **Pinaka bow** | Shiva's bow at Sita's swayamvar; broken by Rama |
| **Ravana's devotion** | Greatest devotee of Shiva; receives Chandrahas sword |
| **Shiva Tandava Stotram** | Composed by Ravana while pinned under Kailash |
| **Nandi's curse** | Lanka to be destroyed by monkeys — foreshadowing |
| **Rameshwaram Jyotirlinga** | Established by Rama himself to expiate Brahmahatya |
| **Hanuman as Shiva** | Shiva's energy serving Vishnu's purpose |
| **Rama's worship** | Vishnu's avatar worshipping Shiva — highest harmony |
