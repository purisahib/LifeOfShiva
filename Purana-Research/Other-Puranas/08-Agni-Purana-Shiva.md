# Agni Purana — Lord Shiva Content

## Basic Information
- **Name:** Agni Purana (the Purana of Fire — spoken by Lord Agni, the fire god)
- **Category:** One of 18 Mahapuranas; classified as **Tamasic** (Shaiva-associated)
- **Verses:** Approximately **15,000 shlokas**
- **Chapters:** 383 chapters
- **Primary Focus:** An **encyclopedic Purana** — covering temple architecture, iconography, grammar, medicine, warfare, poetry, philosophy, and ritual
- **Unique Character:** Often called "a Purana that is also a complete manual of Hindu civilization"
- **Dating:** Composition c. 7th–10th century CE

---

## Why Agni Purana Matters for Shiva

The Agni Purana is not primarily a narrative Purana (it has fewer myths than Shiva Purana or Bhagavata). Instead, it is a **technical manual** — and a massive section of it is devoted to the **correct worship, iconography, and installation of Shiva** in temples.

This makes it extraordinarily valuable: it tells us not just WHO Shiva is, but exactly HOW he was to be depicted in stone, bronze, and ritual practice in ancient India.

---

## 1. Shiva Iconography — The Sacred Manual (Chapters 50–53)

The Agni Purana contains detailed **silpa shastra** (sculpture and architecture) specifications for Shiva images:

### The Shivalinga

**Chapter 50** specifies:
- The Linga must be made in specific proportions — the underground portion (Brahma bhaga), the platform portion (Vishnu bhaga), and the top cylindrical portion (Rudra/Shiva bhaga)
- **The pitha (base)** must be made with a water outlet (pranala) — the water used in abhishekam must flow out to the north or east
- **Measurements:** The height of the Linga above the pitha should ideally be 1/3 of the total length
- **Materials:** Stone (best), metal (copper, gold, silver), clay, crystal, emerald — each carries different energy

### The Anthropomorphic Shiva Forms

The Agni Purana specifies precise **iconometric rules** for sculpting each form of Shiva:

**Sadashiva** (the five-faced form / Panchamukha):
- Five faces facing the five directions (North, South, East, West, Upward)
- Ten arms holding all major weapons and symbols
- Three eyes on each face
- The fifth face (Ishana) faces upward — toward liberation

**Nataraja** (Shiva as Cosmic Dancer):
- Four arms specified precisely:
  - Upper right: holds **Damaru** (hourglass drum — the sound of creation)
  - Upper left: holds **Agni** (fire — the dissolution)
  - Lower right: **Abhaya mudra** (the gesture of "fear not")
  - Lower left: pointing down toward the lifted foot (**Gaja hasta** — "elephant's trunk")
- Right foot on **Apasmara** (the demon of ignorance)
- Left foot raised in the dance posture
- Ring of fire (**Prabha mandala**) around the entire figure
- The Ganga flowing from his matted hair

**Ardhanarishvara** (Half-Male, Half-Female):
- Right half = Shiva (depicted with third eye, crescent moon, serpent, ash marks, tiger skin)
- Left half = Parvati (depicted with jewels, lotus, silk, beauty marks, normal eye)
- The union of these halves represents the **Shakti-Shiva principle** — consciousness and energy are inseparable

**Bhairava** (the Terrifying aspect):
- Described with eight arms
- Garland of skulls (Mundamala)
- Standing on a corpse (representing ego)
- Dog as his vahana (vehicle)
- Carrying skull-cup (Kapala) and trident

## 2. Temple Building and Installation Rituals (Chapters 100–104)

The Agni Purana contains the most comprehensive ancient guide to **Shiva temple construction**:

### Site Selection
The text specifies which locations are auspicious for Shiva temples:
- Near sacred rivers (riverbanks, confluences)
- On hilltops
- At the edge of forests
- At sites where spontaneous Lingas have been found (Swayambhu Lingas)

### Prana Pratishtha — The Installation Ritual
The Agni Purana describes the **Prana Pratishtha** ceremony in detail:
1. **Purification of the ground** with mantras and fire ritual
2. **Placement of the sacred diagram** (Vastu Purusha mandala) beneath the foundation
3. **Installation of the Linga** with the 108-name chant (Shatarudriya from Yajurveda)
4. **Opening of the eyes** — the priest uses a golden needle to "open" the eyes of the icon, awakening divine consciousness within it
5. **Daily worship schedule** — the text prescribes 5 or 16 acts of service (Upacharas) to be performed each day

### The Five Daily Offerings

The Agni Purana prescribes the **Panchopachara** (five offerings) for simple daily Shiva worship:
1. **Gandha** — sandalwood paste (for fragrance / Brahma's element)
2. **Pushpa** — flowers (for beauty)
3. **Dhupa** — incense (for purification)
4. **Dipa** — lamp (for light / removing darkness)
5. **Naivedya** — food offering

And for full worship, the **Shodashopachara** (sixteen offerings) beginning with welcoming Shiva as a guest.

## 3. Shiva Mantras and Ritual Instructions

### The Panchakshara in Agni Purana Context

The Agni Purana gives the **ritual context** of Om Namah Shivaya that other texts don't provide:

- **Na** — purifies the sins of the body
- **Ma** — purifies the errors of speech
- **Shi** — purifies the errors of mind
- **Va** — purifies the obstacles in action
- **Ya** — grants liberation

The text recommends chanting the Panchakshara while performing abhishekam (ritual bath) of the Shiva Linga.

### The 1008-Name Worship

The Agni Purana describes a practice where each of Shiva's 1008 names is recited while offering a flower, a bilva leaf, or a drop of water. This forms the backbone of the **Shiva Sahasranama Archana** still practiced in temples today.

## 4. Shiva's Role in Cosmic Philosophy (Chapters 317–327)

These chapters discuss Shiva from a **Vedantic and philosophical perspective**:

### Shiva as the Undivided Consciousness

The text declares:
*"He who is called Shiva by the Shaivas, Vishnu by the Vaishnavas, Brahma by others, Shakti by the Shaktas — he is the one undivided consciousness that pervades everything."*

### The Three Functions

The Agni Purana presents the **Trimurti** (three functions of the One God) with unusual clarity:
- **Brahma** creates — but creation requires the initial seed (which comes from Shiva/Rudra)
- **Vishnu** preserves — but preservation requires consciousness and grace (which comes from Shiva)
- **Shiva** dissolves — but dissolution is not destruction; it is return to the source

The metaphor used: *"As a wave arises from the ocean, lives for a moment, and returns to the ocean — so creation arises from Shiva, lives in Vishnu's care, and dissolves back into Shiva."*

## 5. Medicinal and Protective Uses of Shiva Symbols

The Agni Purana bridges Ayurveda (medicine) and Shaivism in unusual ways:

- **Ash (Vibhuti)** from Shiva's fire is described as having purifying properties — it protects against disease, evil, and negative forces
- **Rudraksha beads** (Shiva's tears) are described with detailed healing properties — each type (1-faced to 14-faced) corresponds to different organs and ailments
- The **Mahamrityunjaya Mantra** is specifically recommended as a healing mantra — to be chanted over water, which is then given to the sick

---

## 6. Shiva in Warfare — The Pashupatastra

The Agni Purana has a section on divine weapons (Divyastras), and provides details on the **Pashupatastra** — Shiva's most powerful weapon:

- Can only be invoked by those who have pleased Shiva
- Once released, cannot be countered by any other weapon
- Destroys not just the target but anything within a vast radius — therefore it must never be used against lesser enemies
- Arjuna received this weapon from Shiva in the forest (Kiratarjuniya episode of Mahabharata)
- The text warns: *"One who possesses the Pashupatastra becomes responsible for the cosmic balance. He must not use it in anger or pride."*

## 7. Shiva Worship Calendar

The Agni Purana specifies which days and times are most sacred for Shiva worship:

| Day/Time | Significance |
|----------|-------------|
| **Pradosha Vrata** | 13th lunar day (Trayodashi) — evening twilight worship |
| **Shivratri** | 14th lunar day (Chaturdashi) of every month — especially Maha Shivratri |
| **Mondays** | Somvara — Shiva's special day (as Someshvara = lord of the Moon) |
| **Shravan month** | The entire month of Shravan (July-August) — most sacred for Shiva |
| **Ashtami** | 8th lunar day — Bhairava's day |
| **Uttarayan** | Winter solstice — when Shiva is said to be most approachable |

---

## 8. The Agni Purana on Liberation Through Shiva Worship

The text concludes its Shaiva sections with:

*"Among all paths — karma (action), jnana (knowledge), bhakti (devotion) — the worship of Shiva through all three simultaneously is the supreme path. One who builds a Shiva temple attains liberation. One who installs a Linga attains liberation. One who protects an existing Shiva temple from harm attains liberation. But above all this: one who simply remembers Shiva with love at the moment of death — that one is liberated."*
