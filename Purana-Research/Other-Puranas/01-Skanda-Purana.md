# Skanda Purana — Lord Shiva Content

## Basic Information
- **Name:** Skanda Purana (named after Skanda/Kartikeya, son of Shiva)
- **Category:** One of the 18 Mahapuranas
- **Size:** **81,000+ verses** — the **LARGEST of all Puranas**
- **Ranked:** 13th in the list of 18 Puranas
- **Language:** Sanskrit
- **Author:** Attributed to Vedavyas
- **Tradition:** Shaivism (Shiva-centered)

---

## Structure — Seven Parts (Khandas)
1. **Maheshwara Khanda** — stories of the Great Lord (Shiva)
2. **Vaishnava Khanda** — Vishnu-related sections
3. **Brahma Khanda** — creation stories
4. **Kashi Khanda** — the holy city of Varanasi (Shiva's own city)
5. **Avanti Khanda** — sacred site Ujjain (Mahakaleshwar)
6. **Nagar Khanda** — various sacred cities
7. **Prabhasa Khanda** — includes Somnath (Jyotirlinga)

---

## Major Sections with Shiva Content

### Maheshwara Khanda (Part 1)
This is the primary Shiva-focused section:
- **Nature and qualities of Shiva** as the Absolute
- Source of all true knowledge
- Shiva as the **Parameshvara** (Supreme Lord)
- Stories of Shiva's grace to devotees

### Kashi Khanda (Part 4) — The Glory of Varanasi
One of the most detailed sections about **Varanasi/Kashi** — Shiva's earthly dwelling:
- Varanasi is **never abandoned by Shiva** — he resides there eternally
- Every soul dying in Kashi receives Shiva's **Taraka Mantra** (liberating mantra) whispered in their ear
- Description of the 56 Vinayakas (Ganeshas) of Varanasi
- The 108 Shiva lingas of Varanasi
- The Panchakoshi Yatra (circumambulation of the city)
- Stories of Shiva as **Vishwanath** (Lord of the Universe) in Kashi
- **Annapurna** (Parvati as the goddess of food) feeds all in Kashi

### Prabhasa Khanda (Part 7) — Somnath
- Story of the **Somnath Jyotirlinga** and its origins
- Moon God's (Chandra's) curse and liberation through Shiva

## Major Narratives About Shiva in Skanda Purana

### 1. Daksha's Sacrifice
- The complete story of Daksha's yajna
- Sati's self-immolation
- Shiva's grief and the destruction of Daksha's yajna by Virabhadra

### 2. The Churning of the Ocean
- Shiva's drinking of the Halahala poison
- His transformation into Neelkanth (blue-throated one)
- His role in saving all creation

### 3. Birth of Goddess Parvati
- Sati's reincarnation as the mountain princess
- Her childhood devotion to Shiva

### 4. Parvati's Pursuit of Shiva
- Her tapasya (penance) in the Himalayas
- The burning of Kamadeva
- Shiva's tests of Parvati's devotion
- Their marriage

### 5. Birth of Kartikeya
- The demon Tarakasura's terror
- Shiva's divine seed passed through Agni (fire) to the Ganga
- Nurturing by the six Krittikas
- Kartikeya's birth with six heads
- His conquest of Tarakasura

### 6. Sati Character — Full Story
- Complete narrative of Sati's life, marriage, and death
- Shiva's grief
- The 51 Shakti Pithas

## The Kashi Khanda — Special Significance for Writers

*Most detailed Purana section on Shiva's earthly abode*

The Kashi Khanda describes Varanasi as the intersection of the physical and spiritual:
- Kashi is described as Shiva's own **trident** tip — the three prongs are three neighborhoods
- The **Ganga** flows northward through Kashi — this is unusual and sacred
- The city is believed to exist **beyond the physical world** — it is held on Shiva's trident, suspended above earth
- When the universe dissolves, Kashi alone survives — it is taken up by Shiva himself

*"He who dies in Kashi achieves liberation not through merit but through grace alone — for Shiva himself whispers the Taraka Mantra in the dying person's ear."*

## Pilgrimage Significance
The Skanda Purana was used as a **pilgrimage guide** throughout South Asia — it details:
- Sacred sites (tirthas) across India
- Routes and rituals for each pilgrimage
- Stories explaining why each site is holy
- Blessings earned by visiting each place

---

## Skanda (Kartikeya) — The Central Hero
Though named after Skanda, the Purana is fundamentally Shaiva:
- Skanda = **son of Shiva** — his story demonstrates Shiva's power to create a divine warrior
- Skanda's victory over Tarakasura = Shiva's power acting through his offspring
- **Skanda = the invincible warrior born of Shiva's consciousness**

Tamil names: Murugan, Kumaran, Subrahmanya
Epithets: Shadanana (six-faced), Devasena-pati (commander of divine armies), Guha (the hidden one)
