# Shiva Purana - Overview and Structure

## Basic Information
- **Full Name:** Shiva Purana (Sanskrit: Śivapurāṇa / Śivamahāpurāṇa)
- **Category:** One of the 18 Mahapuranas of Hinduism
- **Author:** Attributed to Maharishi Vedavyas
- **Ranking:** Fourth among the 18 major Hindu Puranas
- **Total Verses:** Approximately 24,000 verses (some versions claim up to 100,000)
- **Primary Focus:** Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati
- **Dating:** Oldest chapters likely 10th-11th century CE; some sections after 14th century

## Seven Samhitas — Detailed Content

### 1. Vidyesvara Samhita
- Focuses on **Shiva worship practices**
- Explains the significance of the **Shiva Linga** (phallic emblem)
- Discusses the **Panchakritya** (five-fold cosmic activities): Creation, Sustenance, Destruction, Concealment, Grace
- Meditation on OM and its three components (A, U, M)
- Proper conduct for devotees
- References **Shaiva Agamas** and Vedic texts
- Discusses saguna (with attributes) and nirguna (without attributes) aspects of Shiva

### 2. Rudra Samhita (Parts 1-5)
- **Creation mythology** — origin of Brahma and Vishnu from Shiva's divine energy
- **Story of Sati's self-immolation** at Daksha's yajna
- **Shiva's grief** and his wandering with Sati's body
- **Parvati's penance** and rebirth as Shiva's consort
- **Marriage of Shiva and Parvati**
- **Birth of Kartikeya** (Skanda/Murugan)
- Battles against demons: **Tripura**, **Jalandhara**, **Shankhachuda**
- Story of the **pillar of fire (Analstambh)** manifestation
- Narada's spiritual journey and ego subduing

### 3. Satarudra Samhita
- **Five incarnations of the Supreme Brahman** as Shiva
- Various manifestations and forms of Shiva
- Description of Shiva's divine attributes

### 4. Kotirudra Samhita
- **The Twelve Jyotirlingas** — sacred shrines where Shiva manifested as light
- **Shiva Sahasranama** — the 1000 names of Lord Shiva
- **Shivaratri** observances and their significance
- Importance of Shiva worship

### 5. Uma Samhita
- Ethics and philosophy
- Stories about Goddess Uma/Parvati

### 6. Kailas Samhita
- **Initiation practices**
- **Yoga** teachings
- Description of Mount Kailash

### 7. Vayaviya Samhita
- **Creation cosmology**
- **Devotional teachings**
- **Shaiva-Advaita philosophy** — Brahman as Satcitananda
- Masculine and feminine Shiva-Shakti as unity
- Emphasis on bhakti and yoga over bookish Vedic learning
- "Love-driven devotionalism (bhakti) leads to knowledge"

## Core Themes

### Philosophy
- Blends **Advaita Vedanta** non-dualism with **theistic bhakti** devotionalism
- Shiva presented as the **universal atman** (soul)
- Shiva as the unchanging metaphysical reality and innermost essence of existence
- The Brahman as **Satcitananda** (Being-Consciousness-Bliss)

### Key Teachings
- Devotion (bhakti) to Shiva leads to **moksha** (liberation)
- The five cosmic duties: **creation, sustenance, destruction, concealment, grace**
- Significance of **Linga worship**
- The path: karma-yajna → tapo-yajna → self-study → meditation → jnana-yajna → sayujya (intimate union with Shiva)
- In Kali Yuga: liberation through **devotion** rather than Vedic knowledge

### Key Narratives
1. Origin of Brahma and Vishnu from Shiva's divine energy
2. The infinite pillar of fire (Shiva's manifestation as Jyotirlinga)
3. Sati's self-immolation and Shiva's grief
4. Parvati's birth, penance, and marriage to Shiva
5. Birth of Kartikeya (son of Shiva)
6. Destruction of Tripura (three demon cities)
7. Battle with Jalandhara
8. Battle with Shankhachuda
9. The Twelve Jyotirlingas
10. Shiva's role in Samudra Manthan (Neelkanth story)

## Seven Sections — Chapter-by-Chapter Summary (WisdomLib)

### Section 1 — Vidyeśvara-saṃhitā (25 chapters)
Doubts of sages, excellence of listening, the phallic emblem (liṅga), creation narratives, modes of worship, holy centers, sacred ashes, and Rudrākṣa beads.

### Section 2.1 — Rudra-saṃhitā Part 1: Sṛṣṭi-khaṇḍa (20 chapters)
Creation narratives, Nārada's journey, disputes between celestial beings, the nature of Śivatattva, friendship between Śiva and Kubera.

### Section 2.2 — Rudra-saṃhitā Part 2: Satī-khaṇḍa (43 chapters)
Satī's life narrative, Kāma's appearance and curse, Dakṣa's sacrifice, the divine marriage of Śiva and Satī, Vīrabhadra's emergence.

### Section 2.3 — Rudra-saṃhitā Part 3: Pārvatī-khaṇḍa (55 chapters)
Pārvatī's birth and penance, her marriage to Śiva, testing of her devotion, elaborate wedding ceremonies on Mount Kailāsa.

### Section 2.4 — Rudra-saṃhitā Part 4: Kumāra-khaṇḍa (20 chapters)
Kārttikeya's birth and exploits, Gaṇeśa's origins and coronation, various divine conflicts.

### Section 2.5 — Rudra-saṃhitā Part 5: Yuddha-khaṇḍa (59 chapters)
Battles including the **destruction of Tripura**, conflicts with **Jalandhara** and **Śaṅkhacūḍa**, celestial warfare.

### Section 3 — Śatarudra-saṃhitā (42 chapters)
Śiva's incarnations: **Bhairava**, Nṛsiṃha, Śarabha, Durvāsas, Hanūmat, Kirāta. Also covers the **twelve Jyotirliṅga** manifestations.

### Section 4 — Koṭirudra-Saṃhitā (43 chapters)
Greatness of Jyotirliṅgas, **Śiva-sahasranāma** (thousand names of Shiva), **Śivarātri** observance, philosophical distinctions between qualified and unqualified Brahman.

### Section 5 — Umā-Saṃhitā (51 chapters)
Dialogues with Krishna, descriptions of sins and hells, geographic cosmology (Jambūdvīpa and continents), creation accounts, incarnations of the divine feminine, ethical teachings.

### Section 6 — Kailāsa-saṃhitā (23 chapters)
Renunciation practices, ascetic conduct, Śiva worship techniques, meditative interpretation of sacred syllables (Om Namah Shivaya), funeral rites for renunciates.

### Section 7.1 — Vāyavīya-saṃhitā Part 1 (35 chapters)
Time's nature, creation principles, Dakṣa's sacrifice destruction, Kāma's burning, Pārvatī's ascetic practices, teaching of Upamanyu.

### Section 7.2 — Vāyavīya-saṃhitā Part 2 (41 chapters)
Krishna's devotion to Śiva, the five-syllabled mantra's significance, initiation procedures, ritual practices, yoga teachings, philosophical instruction.

## Preliminary Mahatmya Section (7 chapters)
Introduction establishing the Purana's greatness, narrative accounts of liberation through its teachings, and guidelines for proper recitation.

## Appendix
Discussion of Śiva's five faces (pañcānana).
