# The 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva — Complete Guide

## What is a Jyotirlinga?
**Jyoti** = Light/Flame | **Linga** = Symbol/Form of Shiva

A Jyotirlinga is a sacred shrine where Lord Shiva manifested himself as an **infinite pillar of blazing light** — symbolizing his formless, infinite, eternal nature. These are the holiest temples of Lord Shiva in India.

**Total:** 12 Jyotirlingas across India

---

## The Origin Story (from Shiva Purana)
Brahma and Vishnu argued about who was the supreme god. Lord Shiva manifested as a **pillar of infinite light** and challenged them to find its beginning or end.

- **Vishnu** dived as a boar deep into the earth — could not find the base
- **Brahma** flew as a swan into the sky — could not find the top

**Vishnu admitted defeat honestly.**
**Brahma lied** — he claimed to have seen the top and brought a Ketaki flower as false witness.

Shiva emerged and:
- **Cursed Brahma** — never to be worshipped in temples
- **Cursed Ketaki flower** — never to be used in Shiva worship
- **Blessed Vishnu** — honoring his honesty

The 12 places where this divine pillar of light appeared became the Jyotirlingas.

## The 12 Jyotirlingas — Complete List

### 1. SOMNATH — Gujarat
- **Location:** Prabhas Patan, Saurashtra, Gujarat (on the western coast)
- **Legend:** The Moon God (**Chandra**) had 27 wives (all daughters of Daksha). He showed favoritism to Rohini and neglected others. Enraged, Daksha cursed Chandra — his light would diminish day by day (this explains the waxing and waning of the moon). Chandra prayed to Shiva here. Shiva granted him partial relief — his light wanes for 15 days and then returns for 15 days.
- **Name meaning:** Soma = Moon; Nath = Lord. "Lord of the Moon"
- **Significance:** First and most ancient Jyotirlinga. Destroyed and rebuilt 17 times by invaders.

### 2. MALLIKARJUNA — Andhra Pradesh
- **Location:** Srisailam, Kurnool district, Andhra Pradesh (on the banks of Krishna River)
- **Legend:** When Kartikeya left his parents in anger over who should marry first, Shiva and Parvati visited Srisailam to console him. Their divine presence consecrated the spot. Shiva manifested as Mallikarjuna here.
- **Name meaning:** Mallika = Jasmine; Arjuna = White/Noble. Parvati is Mallika (jasmine goddess), Shiva is Arjuna (white jasmine/noble). Together: Mallikarjuna.
- **Significance:** Visiting this is equivalent to attaining moksha.

### 3. MAHAKALESHWAR — Madhya Pradesh
- **Location:** Ujjain (ancient Avantika), Madhya Pradesh
- **Legend:** A demon named **Dushana** was terrorizing the devotees of Shiva in Ujjain. The citizens prayed intensely to Shiva. Shiva emerged from the ground as a fierce, blazing form and destroyed Dushana and his army in seconds. He then settled there permanently as **Mahakaleshwar** — the Lord of Time and Death.
- **Unique Feature:** The **only Jyotirlinga that is Swayambhu** (self-manifested, not installed by humans) and faces **south** (all other Shiva lingas face east). Southward-facing = lord of death/time.
- **Special Ritual:** The **Bhasma Aarti** — the linga is worshipped with ashes (bhasma) every morning before dawn. The ashes are said to be from cremation grounds.
- **Name meaning:** Maha = Great; Kala = Time/Death; Eshwar = God. "Great God of Time."

### 4. OMKARESHWAR — Madhya Pradesh
- **Location:** Mandhata Island in the Narmada River, Khandwa district, Madhya Pradesh
- **Legend:** The island is **shaped like the Sanskrit letter OM** when viewed from above. King Mandhata of the Ikshvaku dynasty performed tapasya here and Shiva was pleased, appearing as Omkareshwar.
- **Special Feature:** Two lingas — Omkareshwar and Mamleshwar (Amareshwar) — are counted as one Jyotirlinga.
- **Name meaning:** Omkar = OM (the primordial sound); Eshwar = God.

### 5. KEDARNATH — Uttarakhand
- **Location:** Kedarnath, Rudraprayag district, Uttarakhand (at 3,553 meters altitude in the Himalayas)
- **Legend:** After the **Kurukshetra War**, the Pandavas were overcome with guilt for killing their own kin. They sought Shiva's forgiveness. Shiva, wanting to avoid them, took the form of a **bull** and tried to escape into the ground at this spot.
- **Bhima** recognized him and grabbed the bull's hump (back). Shiva vanished, leaving only the **hump above ground** — which became the sacred Shiva Linga worshipped as Kedarnath.
- The remaining body parts appeared at four other locations (collectively called **Panch Kedar**):
  - Arms: **Tungnath**
  - Face: **Rudranath**
  - Navel: **Madhyamaheshwar**
  - Hair: **Kalpeshwar**
- **Name meaning:** Kedar = Field/Dwelling place; Nath = Lord.
- **Significance:** One of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites. Open only 6 months a year (summer).

### 6. BHIMASHANKAR — Maharashtra
- **Location:** Sahyadri Hills, Pune district, Maharashtra (source of the Bhima River)
- **Legend:** A demon named **Bhima** (son of demon Kumbhakarna and a brahmin woman named Karkati) grew enormously powerful. He conquered the gods and imprisoned the king of Kamarupa, Dakshinarayana. Dakshinarayana prayed to Shiva. Shiva appeared in a cosmic battle and destroyed **Bhima** and his demon army. After the battle, the gods requested Shiva to remain. He agreed, and the sweat from his battle-body became the **Bhima River**.
- **Name meaning:** Bhima (the demon defeated here) + Shankar (Shiva).
- **Significance:** The rare **Shekru** (Indian Giant Squirrel, state animal of Maharashtra) is protected in the Bhimashankar forest.

### 7. KASHI VISHWANATH — Uttar Pradesh
- **Location:** Varanasi (Kashi/Benares), Uttar Pradesh — on the western bank of the Ganges
- **Legend:** **Varanasi is the most sacred city on earth** — Shiva's personal city. It is said Shiva never leaves Kashi. Those who die in Kashi receive the **Taraka Mantra** (liberating mantra) whispered in their ear by Shiva himself, guaranteeing moksha regardless of their sins.
- **Name meaning:** Vishwa = Universe; Nath = Lord. "Lord of the Universe."
- **Special Teaching:** Shiva himself is said to preside here as **Dakshinamurti** (the south-facing teacher), teaching the ultimate truths.
- **Significance:** The most visited Jyotirlinga. Bathing in the Ganges here and worshipping Kashi Vishwanath is believed to grant liberation.

### 8. TRIMBAKESHWAR — Maharashtra
- **Location:** Near Nashik, Maharashtra (source of the Godavari River)
- **Legend:** The sage **Gautama** lived here with his wife Ahalya. During a famine, by Shiva's grace, his ashram had abundant food. Jealous sages sent a phantom cow into his fields. When Gautama tried to shoo it away, the cow fell dead. Accused of cow-murder (brahmahatya), Gautama did penance and brought the **Goddess Ganga** (as Godavari) to earth to purify himself. Shiva manifested here as Trimbakeshwar.
- **Unique Feature:** The linga here has **three faces** representing Brahma, Vishnu, and Mahesh (the Trimurti).
- **Name meaning:** Trishula = Trident; Ambaka = Eyes (three-eyed). Or: Tri = Three; Ambak = Eyes = the three-eyed one.

### 9. VAIDYANATH (BAIDYANATH) — Jharkhand
- **Location:** Deoghar, Jharkhand
- **Legend:** **Ravana**, the demon king, performed severe tapasya to Shiva to gain boons. When Shiva still seemed unmoved, Ravana began cutting off his own heads one by one as offerings. As he was about to cut the last head, Shiva appeared, healed him, and granted him tremendous power. It is also said Ravana carried the Shiva Linga here — Shiva had told him not to place it on the ground, but Ravana was tricked by Vishnu's deception into setting it down here.
- **Name meaning:** Vaidya = Physician/Healer; Nath = Lord. "Lord of Physicians" — Shiva heals here.

### 10. NAGESHWAR — Gujarat
- **Location:** Dwarka, Gujarat
- **Legend:** A demon named **Daruka** ruled the sea and kidnapped many Shiva devotees, locking them in his underwater city. A devotee named **Supriya** led fellow prisoners in chanting the Shiva Panchakshara mantra. Shiva emerged and destroyed Daruka and his wife Daruki. He remained there to protect devotees from all serpent-poison and evil.
- **Name meaning:** Naga = Serpent/Snake; Eshwar = God. "God of Serpents" / "The Serpent Lord."
- **Significance:** Shiva here is called the Lord of all serpents, protecting from snake poison.

### 11. RAMESHWARAM — Tamil Nadu
- **Location:** Rameswaram island, Ramanathapuram district, Tamil Nadu (southernmost Jyotirlinga)
- **Legend:** **Lord Rama**, after killing Ravana to rescue Sita, was advised by sages to wash away the sin of killing a brahmin (Ravana was a brahmin). He prayed here and installed a **Shiva Linga** to worship Shiva. Shiva forgave him and blessed the enterprise. The linga was brought by Hanuman from Kailash. But since Hanuman was late, Sita made a small linga from sand — and Shiva declared that the **sand linga made by Sita** would be worshipped first.
- **Name meaning:** Rama (Lord Rama) + Eshwaram (God Shiva) = "Shiva worshipped by Rama."
- **Special Feature:** The corridor with **1,212 pillars** — the longest temple corridor in Asia.
- **Significance:** One of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites.

### 12. GRISHNESHWAR (GHUSHMESHWAR) — Maharashtra
- **Location:** Aurangabad district, Maharashtra (near Ellora Caves)
- **Legend:** A devoted brahmin woman named **Kusuma** (also called Ghushma) worshipped Shiva daily by making a linga from earth, immersing it in a pond, and then reclaiming it. By Shiva's grace she was blessed with a son. Her jealous co-wife killed the son and threw him in the same pond. When Kusuma went for her daily worship, Shiva returned her son alive. Her pure devotion moved Shiva to remain there permanently as **Grishneshwar**.
- **Name meaning:** Ghushma/Grishna = that devotee; Eshwar = God. "God who appeared for Ghushma."

## The Dwadasha Jyotirlinga Shloka
*This Sanskrit shloka lists all 12 Jyotirlingas and is recited at the start of worship:*

> Saurashtre Somanatham cha, Srishaile Mallikarjunam |
> Ujjayinyam Mahakalam, Omkaramam Mamalesvaram ||
> Paralyam Vaidyanatham cha, Dakinyam Bhimashankaram |
> Setubandhe tu Ramesam, Nagesam Darukavane ||
> Varanasyam tu Vishvesha, Tryambakam Gautamitate |
> Himalaye tu Kedaresh, Ghushmesham cha Shivalaye ||

## Spiritual Significance
- Each Jyotirlinga is a **svayambhu** (self-manifested) or divinely established shrine
- Visiting all 12 = **Dwadasha Jyotirlinga Yatra** — one of Hinduism's holiest pilgrimages
- Said to destroy all sins and grant moksha (liberation)
- Span the entire geography of India — North to South, East to West
